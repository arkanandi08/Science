Lithium

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Li
  • Atomic Number: 3
  • Atomic Mass: 6.94 amu
  • Group: 1 (Alkali metals)
  • Period: 2
  • Block: s-block
  • Electron Configuration: (1s22s1)
  • Valence Electrons: 1
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Soft, silvery-white metal
  • Density: 0.534 g/cm³ (the lightest metal)
  • Melting Point: 180.5°C (356.9°F)
  • Boiling Point: 1342°C (2448°F)
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Conductivity: Good conductor of heat and electricity
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Highly reactive, especially in water and air.
    • Reacts with water to produce lithium hydroxide and hydrogen gas
    • Reacts with oxygen to form lithium oxide (Li2O).
  2. Flammability: Burns with a bright red flame in air or oxygen.
  3. Corrosive Behavior: Reacts with nitrogen to form lithium nitride (Li3N).
  4. Forms a protective oxide layer when exposed to air.
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~20 ppm (parts per million)
  2. Found in mineral sources such as: Spodumene (LiAlSi2O6)
Uses of Lithium:-
  1. Batteries: Widely used in lithium-ion batteries for mobile phones, laptops, electric vehicles, and renewable energy storage.
  2. Alloys: Used to make lightweight, strong alloys for the aerospace industry.
  3. Pharmaceuticals: Lithium salts (like lithium carbonate) are used to treat bipolar disorder.
  4. Glass and Ceramics: Improves the strength and thermal resistance of glass and ceramics.
  5. Nuclear Applications: Lithium-6 isotope is used as a coolant and for tritium production in nuclear reactions.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Lightweight: Lithium is the lightest solid element.
  2. Energy Storage: Lithium-ion batteries have revolutionized portable energy storage.
  3. Flame Test: Emits a bright crimson-red flame when burned.
Discovery:
  1. 1817:
    • Johan August Arfvedson, a Swedish chemist, discovered lithium while analyzing the mineral petalite (LiAlSi4O10).
    • Arfvedson found a new element in the mineral but could not isolate it in its pure form.
  2. Name Origin: Lithium gets its name from the Greek word “lithos” meaning “stone,” reflecting its discovery in a mineral rather than in biological or aquatic sources like other alkali metals.
  3. Early Applications:
    • In the 19th century, lithium salts were used as a treatment for gout and later for various mental health conditions.
    • The first widespread industrial use of lithium occurred in lithium-based lubricants during the early 20th century, thanks to its ability to withstand high temperatures.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Lithium was first isolated in 1817 by Johan August Arfvedson.
  2. The name “Lithium” is derived from the Greek word *lithos*, meaning “stone.”
  3. It’s one of the only elements produced during the Big Bang.
