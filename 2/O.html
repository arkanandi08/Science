Oxygen

General Information:-
  • Symbol: O
  • Atomic Number: 8
  • Atomic Mass: 16.00 amu
  • Group: 16 (Chalcogens)
  • Period: 2
  • Block: p-block
  • Electron Configuration: (1s2 2s2 2p4)
  • Valence Electrons: 6
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Colorless, odorless, and tasteless gas.
  • Density: 1.429 g/L (at 0°C and 1 atm).
  • Melting Point: -218.8°C (-361.8°F).
  • Boiling Point: -183°C (-297.4°F).
  • State at Room Temperature: Gas.
  • Conductivity: Non-conductive.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Highly reactive and supports combustion.
    • Forms oxides with most elements, such as water (H2O) and carbon dioxide (CO2).
    • Combines with hydrogen to form hydrogen peroxide (H2O2).
  2. Oxidation States:
    • Common oxidation state: -2 (e.g., in water and oxides).
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~46.6% by mass (most abundant element).
  2. Atmospheric Presence: ~21% by volume.
  3. Found in:
    • Water (H2O).
    • Minerals such as silica (SiO2).
    • Biological molecules like carbohydrates, proteins, and DNA.
Uses of Oxygen:-
  1. Medical Use: Administered for respiratory conditions and life support systems.
  2. Industrial Use:
    • Used in steelmaking and welding.
    • Oxygen-enriched air is used in chemical reactions to enhance combustion.
  3. Rocket Fuel: Combines with hydrogen as an oxidizer in rocket engines.
  4. Water Treatment: Used to purify water in sewage treatment plants.
Biological Role:-
  1. Essential for Life:
    • Required for cellular respiration in humans and animals.
    • Plays a role in photosynthesis in plants, where oxygen is released as a byproduct.
  2. Hemoglobin Transport:
    • In humans, oxygen binds to hemoglobin in red blood cells for transport.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Supports Combustion: Essential for burning fuels.
  2. Allotropic Forms: Exists as diatomic oxygen (O2) and ozone (O3).
  3. Oxidizing Agent: A strong oxidizer, enabling numerous chemical reactions.
History of Oxygen:-
  1. Discovery:
    • Discovered independently by Carl Wilhelm Scheele in 1772 and Joseph Priestley in 1774.
    • The name "oxygen" was coined by Antoine Lavoisier in 1777, meaning "acid former."
  2. Historical Use: Recognized for its role in combustion and respiration.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Abundance: Oxygen is the most abundant element in Earth's crust and the third most abundant in the universe.
  2. Ozone Layer: Protects Earth from harmful ultraviolet radiation by absorbing it.
  3. Liquid Oxygen: Has a pale blue color and is used as a coolant and oxidizer.
