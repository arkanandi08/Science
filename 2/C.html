Carbon

General Information:-
  • Symbol: C
  • Atomic Number: 6
  • Atomic Mass: 12.01 amu
  • Group: 14 (Carbon Group)
  • Period: 2
  • Block: p-block
  • Electron Configuration: (1s2 2s2 2p2)
  • Valence Electrons: 4
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Exists in various forms (allotropes) such as diamond (transparent and crystalline) and graphite (black and opaque).
  • Density:
    • Diamond: ~3.51 g/cm³
    • Graphite: ~2.26 g/cm³
  • Melting Point: Sublimates at ~3550°C (6422°F) under standard pressure (graphite).
  • Boiling Point: ~4830°C (8726°F).
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid.
  • Conductivity:
    • Diamond: Poor conductor of electricity but excellent thermal conductor.
    • Graphite: Good conductor of electricity.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Forms strong covalent bonds with many elements, especially hydrogen, oxygen, and nitrogen.
    • Combines with oxygen to form carbon dioxide (CO2) or carbon monoxide (CO).
    • Reacts with metals to form carbides (e.g., calcium carbide).
  2. Allotropes:
    • Diamond: Hardest natural substance.
    • Graphite: Soft, slippery, and a good lubricant.
    • Fullerenes: Molecules made of carbon atoms in spherical or cylindrical shapes.
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~0.02%.
  2. Commonly found in:
    • Coal, petroleum, and natural gas.
    • Limestone (CaCO3).
    • Carbon dioxide in the atmosphere (~0.04%).
Uses of Carbon:-
  1. Energy Source: Coal, oil, and natural gas are carbon-based fuels.
  2. Steel Production: Used in the form of coke to extract iron from ores.
  3. Electrodes: Graphite is used in batteries and as electrodes in electrolysis.
  4. Lubricants: Graphite is used as a dry lubricant in machinery.
  5. Materials: Diamond is used in cutting tools, while carbon fibers are used in high-strength materials.
  6. Medicine: Carbon isotopes (e.g., carbon-14) are used in radiocarbon dating and medical diagnostics.
Biological Role:-
  1. Backbone of Life: Forms the basis of organic chemistry; present in all living organisms.
  2. Energy Storage: Carbohydrates and fats are carbon-based molecules used for energy.
  3. Role in Photosynthesis: Plants convert CO2 to glucose using sunlight.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Allotropic Forms: Exists in multiple allotropes (diamond, graphite, fullerenes, graphene, etc.) with distinct properties.
  2. High Bonding Ability: Can form long chains and complex structures with itself and other elements.
  3. Role in Climate: Carbon dioxide is a major greenhouse gas affecting Earth's climate.
History of Carbon:-
  1. Ancient Knowledge:
    • Carbon in the form of charcoal has been used since ancient times.
    • Graphite was known and used in early writing instruments (pencils).
  2. Modern Studies: The discovery of fullerenes in 1985 expanded the understanding of carbon allotropes.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Carbon Allotropes:
    • Diamond is the hardest natural substance.
    • Graphene, a single layer of carbon atoms, is one of the strongest and most conductive materials.
  2. Carbon Cycle: Essential for life, carbon continuously cycles through the atmosphere, oceans, and living organisms.
  3. Isotopes: Carbon-12 and Carbon-13 are stable, while Carbon-14 is radioactive and used in dating ancient materials.
