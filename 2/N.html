Nitrogen

General Information:-
  • Symbol: N
  • Atomic Number: 7
  • Atomic Mass: 14.01 amu
  • Group: 15 (Nitrogen Group)
  • Period: 2
  • Block: p-block
  • Electron Configuration: (1s2 2s2 2p3)
  • Valence Electrons: 5
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Colorless, odorless, and tasteless gas.
  • Density: 1.25 g/L (at 0°C and 1 atm).
  • Melting Point: -210°C (-346°F).
  • Boiling Point: -195.8°C (-320.4°F).
  • State at Room Temperature: Gas.
  • Conductivity: Non-conductive.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Relatively inert at room temperature due to its triple bond (N≡N).
    • Combines with oxygen to form nitrogen oxides (e.g., NO, NO2).
    • Forms ammonia (NH3) in the Haber process.
  2. Oxidation States:
    • Common oxidation states: -3 (e.g., NH3), +1, +2, +3, +4, and +5 (e.g., HNO3).
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Atmosphere: ~78% (by volume).
  2. Found in:
    • Nitrates (e.g., KNO3, NaNO3).
    • Ammonium compounds (e.g., NH4Cl).
    • Biological molecules such as DNA, RNA, and proteins.
Uses of Nitrogen:-
  1. Industrial Uses:
    • Used in the Haber process to produce ammonia for fertilizers.
    • Liquid nitrogen is used as a coolant in cryogenics.
  2. Food Preservation: Used to package food to prevent oxidation and spoilage.
  3. Electronics: Used in the production of semiconductors.
  4. Inert Atmosphere: Used in welding and metal processing to prevent oxidation.
Biological Role:-
  1. Essential Element:
    • Vital for the synthesis of amino acids, proteins, nucleic acids, and other biomolecules.
    • Plants utilize nitrogen through nitrogen fixation and nitrates.
  2. Nitrogen Cycle:
    • Key component of the nitrogen cycle, involving processes like nitrogen fixation, nitrification, and denitrification.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Triple Bond Strength: The N≡N bond in molecular nitrogen makes it highly stable and inert.
  2. Abundance: The most abundant gas in Earth's atmosphere.
  3. Role in Explosives: Found in compounds like TNT and nitroglycerin.
History of Nitrogen:-
  1. Discovery:
    • Discovered in 1772 by Scottish chemist Daniel Rutherford.
  2. Ancient Knowledge: Nitrogen compounds like saltpeter (KNO3) were used in gunpowder and fertilizers.
  3. Modern Applications: Nitrogen-based fertilizers and chemicals revolutionized agriculture and industry.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Symbol Origin: "N" comes from the Latin word *nitrum*, meaning "niter" or "saltpeter."
  2. Inert Nature: Its stability makes it useful for creating non-reactive environments.
  3. Liquid Nitrogen: Widely used for freezing biological samples and as a cooling agent.
