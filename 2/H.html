Hydrogen

Basic Information:-
  • Symbol: H
  • Atomic Number: 1
  • Atomic Mass: 1.008 amu
  • Group: 1 (though not officially part of alkali metals)
  • Period: 1
  • Block: s-block
  • Category: Non-metal
  • Electronic configuration: 1s1
  • Valence electrons: 1
Physical Properties:-
  • State at Room Temperature: Gas
  • Color: Colorless
  • Odor: Odorless
  • Density: 0.08988 g/L (at STP)

Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity: Highly reactive. Combines readily with most elements.
  2. Electronegativity: 2.20 (Pauling scale)
  3. Ionization Energy: 1312 kJ/mol
  4. Common Oxidation States: +1 (in compounds like H2O), -1 (in hydrides like NaH).
  5. Flammability: Highly flammable, burns with a pale blue flame forming water.
Abundance:-
  1. Most abundant element in the universe, making up about 75% of normal matter by mass.
  2. Found In: Stars (as plasma), water (H2O), hydrocarbons, and various organic compounds.
  3. On Earth: Found in molecular form (H2) or in compounds like water and natural gas.
Isotopes:-
  1. Protium (H-1): Most common isotope with no neutrons.
  2. Deuterium (H-2 or D): Contains one neutron, used in heavy water and nuclear reactors
    3. .
  3. Tritium (H-3): Contains two neutrons, radioactive, used in nuclear fusion research.
Applications:-
  1. Energy Source: Used in hydrogen fuel cells to generate electricity.
  2. Chemical Industry: Produces ammonia (Haber process), methanol, and hydrogen chloride.
  3. Welding and Cutting: Used as fuel in oxy-hydrogen torches.
  4. Space Exploration: Rocket fuel in the form of liquid hydrogen.
  5. Coolant: Used in generators and some nuclear reactors.
History of Hydrogen:-
  1. Discovery: Hydrogen was discovered by Henry Cavendish in 1766.
  2. Name: The name "hydrogen" comes from the Greek words "hydro" (water) and "genes" (generator).
  3. Early uses: Hydrogen was initially used for lighting and as a lifting gas in balloons.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Hydrogen is the lightest and simplest element, consisting of just one proton and one electron.
  2. Hydrogen was the first element formed after the Big Bang.
  3. Its energy content is nearly three times higher than gasoline by weight, making it an ideal candidate for clean energy.
  4. Liquid hydrogen is extremely cold and requires special storage and handling.
