Thulium

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Tm
  • Atomic Number: 69
  • Atomic Mass: 168.93 amu
  • Group: Lanthanides
  • Period: 6
  • Block: f-block
  • Electron Configuration: [Xe] 4f136s2
  • Valence Electrons: 3
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Silvery-gray, soft metal.
  • Density: 9.32 g/cm³
  • Melting Point: 1,545°C (2,813°F)
  • Boiling Point: 1,950°C (3,542°F)
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Conductivity: Moderate thermal and electrical conductor.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Slowly oxidizes in air, forming a protective oxide layer.
    • Reacts with acids to form thulium salts.
  2. Flame Color: Bright green.
  3. Oxidation States: +3 (most common).
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~0.5 ppm (one of the least abundant lanthanides).
  2. Found in minerals such as monazite and bastnäsite.
Uses of Thulium:-
  1. Medical Imaging: Used in portable X-ray machines.
  2. Lasers: Employed in surgical and industrial lasers.
  3. Electronics: Used in some semiconductor devices.
  4. Nuclear Reactors: Potential use in neutron sources.
  5. High-Performance Magnets: Occasionally used in specialized magnet applications.
Biological Role:-
  1. No known biological function in the human body.
  2. Considered to have low toxicity.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Least Abundant Lanthanide: Thulium is the rarest of the naturally occurring lanthanides.
  2. High-Energy Radiation: Some isotopes of thulium emit X-rays.
  3. Green Flame Test: Thulium compounds produce a bright green flame.
History of Thulium:-
  1. Discovery: Identified in 1879 by Per Teodor Cleve.
  2. Named After: "Thule," an ancient term for Scandinavia.
  3. Modern Applications: Mainly used in medical and laser technologies.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Thulium isotopes are used in nuclear batteries.
  2. Despite its rarity, it has found niche applications in medicine and industry.
  3. Its soft and malleable nature makes it easy to work with in various applications.
