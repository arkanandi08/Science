Lutetium

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Lu
  • Atomic Number: 71
  • Atomic Mass: 174.97 amu
  • Group: Lanthanides
  • Period: 6
  • Block: f-block
  • Electron Configuration: [Xe] 4f145d16s2
  • Valence Electrons: 3
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Silvery-white, hard, and dense metal.
  • Density: 9.84 g/cm³
  • Melting Point: 1,663°C (3,025°F)
  • Boiling Point: 3,402°C (6,156°F)
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Conductivity: Good electrical conductor.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Resistant to oxidation in air.
    • Reacts with acids to form lutetium salts.
  2. Flame Color: Red.
  3. Oxidation State: +3 (most common).
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~0.5 ppm.
  2. Found in minerals such as monazite and xenotime.
Uses of Lutetium:-
  1. Medical Imaging: Used in PET scans and cancer treatments.
  2. Petroleum Industry: Acts as a catalyst in petroleum refining.
  3. Electronics: Used in phosphors and LED technology.
  4. Scientific Research: Employed in experimental physics and nuclear technology.
  5. High-Performance Alloys: Enhances properties of certain metal alloys.
Biological Role:-
  1. No known biological function in the human body.
  2. Considered to have low toxicity.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Densest Lanthanide: Lutetium is the heaviest and hardest lanthanide.
  2. Highly Stable: More resistant to oxidation compared to other lanthanides.
  3. Expensive: One of the rarest and most costly rare earth elements.
History of Lutetium:-
  1. Discovery: Discovered in 1907 by Georges Urbain, Carl Auer von Welsbach, and Charles James.
  2. Named After: Lutetia, the ancient Roman name for Paris.
  3. Modern Applications: Used in medical imaging, catalysts, and cutting-edge technology.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Lutetium was the last of the lanthanides to be discovered.
  2. Its stable isotopes are used in dating meteorites.
  3. Despite being a lanthanide, it has some transition metal-like properties.
