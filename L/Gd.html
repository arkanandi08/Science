Gadolinium

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Gd
  • Atomic Number: 64
  • Atomic Mass: 157.25 amu
  • Group: Lanthanides
  • Period: 6
  • Block: f-block
  • Electron Configuration: [Xe] 4f75d16s2
  • Valence Electrons: 3
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Silvery-white metal that is malleable and ductile.
  • Density: 7.90 g/cm³
  • Melting Point: 1,313°C (2,395°F)
  • Boiling Point: 3,273°C (5,923°F)
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Conductivity: Moderate electrical conductivity.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Moderately reactive and forms an oxide layer in air.
    • Slowly reacts with water, forming gadolinium hydroxide.
    • Forms various compounds such as gadolinium chloride and gadolinium oxide.
  2. Flame Color: Bright white.
  3. Oxidation States: +3 (most common).
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~6.2 ppm.
  2. Found in minerals like monazite and bastnäsite.
Uses of Gadolinium:-
  1. Medical Imaging: Used as a contrast agent in MRI scans.
  2. Neutron Absorption: Used in nuclear reactors to absorb neutrons.
  3. Magnetic Refrigeration: Utilized in cryogenic applications.
  4. Electronics: Used in manufacturing phosphors for color TVs and LEDs.
  5. Data Storage: Enhances properties of compact discs and computer memory devices.
Biological Role:-
  1. No known biological function in the human body.
  2. Gadolinium-based compounds are used in medical imaging but must be handled carefully due to potential toxicity.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Magnetic Properties: Exhibits strong paramagnetism.
  2. Neutron Absorption: Excellent ability to absorb neutrons, making it useful in nuclear reactors.
  3. Low-Temperature Magnetism: Used in specialized cryogenic applications.
History of Gadolinium:-
  1. Discovery: Discovered in 1880 by Jean Charles Galissard de Marignac.
  2. Named After: The mineral gadolinite, which was named after Finnish chemist Johan Gadolin.
  3. Modern Applications: Extensively used in medical imaging, nuclear energy, and electronic industries.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Gadolinium has one of the highest neutron absorption cross-sections of any element.
  2. It is used to enhance image contrast in MRI scans.
  3. Gadolinium-based phosphors improve the performance of LED screens and fluorescent lamps.
