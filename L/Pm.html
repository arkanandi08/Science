Promethium

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Pm
  • Atomic Number: 61
  • Atomic Mass: ~145 amu (radioactive, no stable isotopes)
  • Group: Lanthanides
  • Period: 6
  • Block: f-block
  • Electron Configuration: [Xe] 4f56s2
  • Valence Electrons: 3
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Metallic, but rarely observed due to its radioactivity.
  • Density: ~7.26 g/cm³
  • Melting Point: ~1,104°C (2,019°F)
  • Boiling Point: ~3,000°C (5,432°F) (estimated)
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Conductivity: Good conductor of electricity and heat.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Highly radioactive and unstable.
    • Oxidizes in air, forming a pink or reddish oxide layer.
    • Reacts with acids, forming promethium salts.
  2. Flame Color: Not well studied due to radioactivity.
  3. Oxidation States: +3 (most common).
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: Extremely rare, found only in trace amounts.
  2. Produced synthetically in nuclear reactors and found in uranium ore as a decay product.
Uses of Promethium:-
  1. Radioactive Batteries: Used in space probes and military applications.
  2. Luminous Paint: Previously used in glow-in-the-dark applications.
  3. Medical Research: Investigated for cancer treatments.
Biological Role:-
  1. No known biological role.
  2. Highly radioactive and toxic if ingested or inhaled.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Radioactive: No stable isotopes exist.
  2. Artificially Produced: Mainly obtained from nuclear reactors.
  3. Rare Element: One of the rarest naturally occurring lanthanides.
History of Promethium:-
  1. Discovery: First identified in 1945 by Jacob Marinsky, Lawrence Glendenin, and Charles Coryell.
  2. Named After: Prometheus, the Titan who stole fire from the gods in Greek mythology.
  3. Modern Applications: Used in nuclear batteries and research.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. One of the few elements discovered in the 20th century.
  2. Has no known stable isotopes.
  3. Used in early glow-in-the-dark watches before being replaced by safer materials.
