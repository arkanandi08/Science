Samarium

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Sm
  • Atomic Number: 62
  • Atomic Mass: 150.36 amu
  • Group: Lanthanides
  • Period: 6
  • Block: f-block
  • Electron Configuration: [Xe] 4f66s2
  • Valence Electrons: 3
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Silvery-white metal that tarnishes in air.
  • Density: 7.52 g/cm³
  • Melting Point: 1,072°C (1,962°F)
  • Boiling Point: 1,794°C (3,261°F)
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Conductivity: Moderate conductor of electricity and heat.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Slowly oxidizes in air, forming a yellowish oxide layer.
    • Reacts with acids, releasing hydrogen gas.
    • Combines with halogens to form samarium halides.
  2. Flame Color: Yellowish-green.
  3. Oxidation States: +2, +3 (most common).
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~6 ppm (moderately abundant).
  2. Found in minerals like monazite and bastnäsite.
Uses of Samarium:-
  1. Magnets: Used in samarium-cobalt (SmCo) permanent magnets.
  2. Nuclear Reactors: Acts as a neutron absorber in control rods.
  3. Medicine: Samarium-153 is used in cancer treatment for pain relief in bone cancer.
  4. Optics: Used in special infrared-absorbing glasses.
  5. Catalysts: Employed in various industrial chemical reactions.
Biological Role:-
  1. No known essential biological function.
  2. Considered to have low toxicity but should be handled with care.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Moderate Reactivity: Tarnishes in air but not as rapidly as some lanthanides.
  2. Magnetic Properties: Used in strong, heat-resistant magnets.
  3. Radioactive Isotope: Samarium-153 is used in medical treatments.
History of Samarium:-
  1. Discovery: Identified in 1879 by Paul Émile Lecoq de Boisbaudran.
  2. Named After: The mineral samarskite, which was named after Russian mine official Vasili Samarsky-Bykhovets.
  3. Modern Applications: Integral to nuclear, medical, and magnetic technologies.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. One of the first lanthanides to be isolated.
  2. Samarium-cobalt magnets are used in spacecraft and military applications due to their durability.
  3. It can be used in dating ancient rocks due to its radioactive isotopes.
