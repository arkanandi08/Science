Terbium

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Tb
  • Atomic Number: 65
  • Atomic Mass: 158.93 amu
  • Group: Lanthanides
  • Period: 6
  • Block: f-block
  • Electron Configuration: [Xe] 4f96s2
  • Valence Electrons: 3
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Silvery-white, soft, and malleable metal.
  • Density: 8.23 g/cm³
  • Melting Point: 1,356°C (2,473°F)
  • Boiling Point: 3,123°C (5,653°F)
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Conductivity: Moderate electrical conductivity.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Slowly oxidizes in air, forming an oxide layer.
    • Reacts with acids to form terbium salts.
    • Forms compounds such as terbium oxide (Tb2O3).
  2. Flame Color: Bright green.
  3. Oxidation States: +3 (most common), +4 in some compounds.
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~1.2 ppm.
  2. Found in minerals such as monazite and bastnäsite.
Uses of Terbium:-
  1. Electronics: Used in green phosphors for LED displays and fluorescent lamps.
  2. Magnets: Enhances the properties of neodymium-iron-boron (NdFeB) magnets.
  3. Lasers: Used in solid-state laser materials.
  4. Nuclear Reactors: Used as a neutron absorber.
  5. Medical Imaging: Utilized in X-ray and MRI contrast agents.
Biological Role:-
  1. No known biological function in the human body.
  2. Some terbium compounds are used in biomedical imaging.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Green Phosphorescence: Used in high-efficiency lighting and TV screens.
  2. Magnetic Properties: Enhances the performance of strong permanent magnets.
  3. High Neutron Absorption: Useful in nuclear reactor control rods.
History of Terbium:-
  1. Discovery: Discovered in 1843 by Carl Gustaf Mosander.
  2. Named After: The village of Ytterby, Sweden, where its ore was first found.
  3. Modern Applications: Widely used in electronics, magnets, and nuclear technology.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Terbium's green phosphor is crucial for color TV screens and LED displays.
  2. It is one of four elements named after Ytterby, Sweden.
  3. Despite its scarcity, it has essential applications in advanced technologies.
