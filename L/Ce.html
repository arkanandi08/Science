Cerium

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Ce
  • Atomic Number: 58
  • Atomic Mass: 140.12 amu
  • Group: Lanthanides
  • Period: 6
  • Block: f-block
  • Electron Configuration: [Xe] 4f15d16s2
  • Valence Electrons: 4
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Silvery-white, soft metal.
  • Density: 6.770 g/cm³
  • Melting Point: 795°C (1463°F)
  • Boiling Point: 3443°C (6229°F)
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Conductivity: Good conductor of electricity and heat.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Highly reactive, tarnishes in air.
    • Reacts with water, forming cerium hydroxide and hydrogen gas.
    • Forms oxides CeO2 and Ce2O3.
  2. Flame Color: Burns with a bright white spark.
  3. Oxidation States: +3 and +4 (most stable).
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~0.0046% (most abundant rare earth element).
  2. Commonly found as compounds, such as:
    • Monazite (Ce, La, Nd, Th)PO4
    • Bastnäsite (Ce, La, Nd)(CO3F)
Uses of Cerium:-
  1. Catalysts: Used in catalytic converters to reduce vehicle emissions.
  2. Glass Polishing: Cerium oxide is used for polishing glass and screens.
  3. Alloys: Added to aluminum and magnesium alloys for strength.
  4. Lighting: Used in carbon arc lamps for film projection.
  5. Medical: Used in self-cleaning ovens and as an antioxidant in medicine.
  6. Electronics: Used in semiconductors and phosphors for screens.
Biological Role:-
  1. Non-essential for life but can be found in trace amounts in some organisms.
  2. Can act as a mild biological stimulant in some bacteria.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Most Abundant Rare Earth Element.
  2. Oxidizes Readily: Tarnishes when exposed to air.
  3. Sparks When Scraped: Used in flints for lighters.
History of Cerium:-
  1. Discovery: Discovered in 1803 by Jöns Jakob Berzelius and Wilhelm Hisinger (independently by Martin Heinrich Klaproth).
  2. Named After: The dwarf planet Ceres, discovered in 1801.
  3. Early Uses:
    • Initially used in gas mantles and lighter flints.
  4. Modern Applications: Widely used in polishing, catalysts, and electronics.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Named after the planet Ceres.
  2. First lanthanide to be discovered.
  3. Used in self-cleaning ovens due to its oxidation properties.
