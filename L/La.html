Lanthanum

General Information:-
  • Symbol: La
  • Atomic Number: 57
  • Atomic Mass: 138.91 amu
  • Group: Lanthanides
  • Period: 6
  • Block: f-block
  • Electron Configuration: [Xe] 5d16s2
  • Valence Electrons: 3
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Silvery-white, soft, and ductile metal.
  • Density: 6.162 g/cm³.
  • Melting Point: 920°C (1688°F).
  • Boiling Point: 3464°C (6267°F).
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid.
  • Conductivity: Good conductor of electricity and heat.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Reactive, especially with water and air.
    • Forms lanthanum oxide (La2O3) when exposed to oxygen.
    • Reacts with water to form lanthanum hydroxide (La(OH)3).
  2. Flame Color: Produces a faint white flame.
  3. Corrosive Behavior: Tarnishes in air and reacts with acids.
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~0.0034% (one of the most abundant rare-earth elements).
  2. Commonly found in minerals such as:
    • Monazite.
    • Bastnäsite.
Uses of Lanthanum:-
  1. Alloys: Used in nickel-metal hydride (NiMH) batteries.
  2. Optics: Essential in high-quality optical glass and camera lenses.
  3. Catalysts: Used in petroleum refining and catalytic converters.
  4. Electronics: Found in carbon arc lamps and phosphors for screens.
  5. Medical Applications: Used in some cancer treatments and imaging.
Biological Role:-
  1. Non-essential for life: No known biological role.
  2. Medical Use: Lanthanum carbonate is used to treat kidney disease by reducing phosphate absorption.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Softest Lanthanide: Easily cut with a knife.
  2. High Ductility: Can be drawn into thin wires.
  3. Glass Stabilizer: Improves the durability and clarity of glass.
History of Lanthanum:-
  1. Discovery: Discovered in 1839 by Carl Gustav Mosander.
  2. Isolation: First isolated from cerium nitrate.
  3. Industrial Use: Gained importance in 20th-century electronics and battery technology.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Lanthanum means "to lie hidden" in Greek, as it was hidden within cerium minerals.
  2. Used in hybrid car batteries due to its role in NiMH batteries.
  3. Lanthanum oxide is key to camera and telescope lenses.
