Holmium

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Ho
  • Atomic Number: 67
  • Atomic Mass: 164.93 amu
  • Group: Lanthanides
  • Period: 6
  • Block: f-block
  • Electron Configuration: [Xe] 4f116s2
  • Valence Electrons: 3
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Silvery-white, relatively soft metal.
  • Density: 8.79 g/cm³
  • Melting Point: 1,474°C (2,685°F)
  • Boiling Point: 2,700°C (4,892°F)
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Conductivity: Moderate electrical and thermal conductivity.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Slowly oxidizes in air, forming a yellowish oxide layer.
    • Reacts with acids to form holmium salts.
    • Forms compounds such as holmium oxide (Ho2O3).
  2. Flame Color: Yellowish-orange.
  3. Oxidation States: +3 (most common).
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~1.3 ppm.
  2. Found in minerals such as monazite and bastnäsite.
Uses of Holmium:-
  1. Magnets: Used in creating the strongest magnetic fields.
  2. Nuclear Reactors: Employed in control rods due to high neutron absorption.
  3. Lasers: Used in solid-state lasers for medical and industrial applications.
  4. Glass and Ceramics: Adds yellow or red coloring to glass and cubic zirconia.
  5. Data Storage: Utilized in specialized hard disk drives and memory devices.
Biological Role:-
  1. No known biological function in the human body.
  2. Some holmium compounds are studied for potential medical applications.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Magnetic Properties: Has the highest magnetic moment of any naturally occurring element.
  2. High Neutron Absorption: Essential for nuclear reactor control rods.
  3. Laser Efficiency: Used in medical and surgical laser treatments.
History of Holmium:-
  1. Discovery: Identified in 1878 by Per Teodor Cleve.
  2. Named After: "Holmia," the Latin name for Stockholm, Sweden.
  3. Modern Applications: Used in magnets, lasers, and nuclear reactors.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Holmium has the highest magnetic strength of any element.
  2. Despite its strength, it is relatively rare and mainly extracted from monazite ores.
  3. Its laser applications include medical treatments for kidney stones and cancer therapy.
