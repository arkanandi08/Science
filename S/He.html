Helium

Basic Information:-
  • Symbol: He
  • Atomic Number: 2
  • Atomic Mass: 4.002602 u
  • Element Category: Noble Gas
  • Block: s-block
  • Period: 1
  • Electronic configuration: 1s2
  • Valence Electrons: 2
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Colorless, odorless, tasteless gas
  • Phase at Room Temperature: Gas
  • Boiling Point: -268.93°C (-452.07°F)
  • Melting Point: -272.2°C (-458.0°F)
  • Density: 0.1786 g/l
  • Viscosity: 0.0225 mPa·s (millipascal-seconds)
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity: Noble gas, unreactive
  2. Electronegativity: 0 (Pauling scale)
  3. Common Compounds: None, due to its unreactive nature
  4. Reactions: Helium does not react with other elements under normal conditions.
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance: Second most abundant element in the universe (23% by mass) Sources:
  2. Natural Gas: Helium is extracted from natural gas deposits.
  3. Radioactive Decay: Helium is produced through the radioactive decay of uranium and thorium.
Isotopes:-
  1. Helium-3 (He-3):
    • A rare and stable isotope.
    • Nucleus contains two protons and one neutron.
    • Used in nuclear fusion research.
  2. Helium-4 (He-4):
    • The most abundant isotope.
    • Nucleus contains two protons and two neutrons.
    • Used in balloons, airships, and cryogenic applications.
Uses:-
  1. Balloons and Airships: Helium is used as a lifting gas due to its lighter-than-air properties.
  2. Cryogenics: Helium is used as a coolant for superconducting materials and supercolliders.
  3. Medical Imaging: Helium is used as a coolant for superconducting magnets in MRI machines.
  4. Space Exploration: Helium is used as a propellant for rockets and as a coolant for spacecraft.
  5. Nuclear Fusion Research: Helium-3 is used as a fuel in nuclear fusion research.
History:-
  1. Discovery: Helium was discovered by Jules Janssen and Norman Lockyer in 1868.
  2. Name: The name "Helium" comes from the Greek word "helios," meaning sun.
  3. First Isolation: Helium was first isolated by William Ramsay in 1895.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Only Element That Cannot Be Solidified:
    • Helium is the only element that cannot be solidified at normal pressure.
    • It requires a pressure of at least 25 atm to solidify at a temperature of 1.76 K (-271.39°C or -456.5°F).
  2. Superfluidity: Helium-4 becomes a superfluid at temperatures below 2.17 K (-270.98°C or -455.76°F), exhibiting unusual properties such as zero viscosity.
  3. Helium Shortage: The world's helium supply is dwindling, and conservation efforts are underway to reduce waste and increase recycling.
