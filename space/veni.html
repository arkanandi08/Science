Venus

Classification:

Terrestrial Planet

Discovery & History:

Known since ancient times; named after the Roman goddess of love and beauty.

Size & Structure:

Similar in size to Earth, with a diameter of 12,104 km; composed mainly of a rocky mantle and an iron core.

Atmosphere & Climate:

Thick atmosphere of carbon dioxide with clouds of sulfuric acid; surface temperature around 465°C, making it the hottest planet.

Orbital Details:

Orbits the Sun in 225 Earth days; rotates extremely slowly and in the opposite direction of most planets (one Venus day = 243 Earth days).

Moons & Rings:

No moons or rings.

Notable Features:

Surface covered with vast volcanic plains, highland regions, and massive craters; features the longest-known lava channels.

Habitability & Exploration:

Extreme greenhouse effect makes it uninhabitable; explored by probes like Venera, Magellan, and Parker Solar Probe.

Fun Facts:

Venus is sometimes called Earth’s twin due to its similar size, but its conditions are vastly different. It has the slowest rotation of any planet in the solar system.
