Uranus

Classification: Ice Giant

Discovery & History: Discovered by William Herschel in 1781; named after the Greek god of the sky.

Size & Structure: Seventh-largest planet, diameter of 50,724 km; composed mainly of hydrogen, helium, and icy elements like water, methane, and ammonia.

Atmosphere & Climate: Mostly hydrogen and helium with methane, giving it a blue-green color; coldest planet, with temperatures as low as -224Â°C.

Orbital Details: Orbits the Sun in 84 Earth years; rotates on its side with an axial tilt of 98 degrees, making its seasons extreme.

Moons & Rings: 27 known moons, including Titania and Oberon; has faint ring systems.

Notable Features: Unique sideways rotation, icy atmosphere, and extreme winds reaching up to 900 km/h.

Habitability & Exploration: Uninhabitable due to extreme cold and lack of a solid surface; visited only once by Voyager 2 in 1986.

Fun Facts: Uranus is the only planet that rotates on its side, and its seasons last 21 years each!
