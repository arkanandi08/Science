The Sun

Classification:

Main-sequence star (G-type, Yellow Dwarf)

Discovery & History:

The Sun has been observed since ancient times and is central to many mythologies and scientific studies.

Size & Structure:

Diameter of about 1.39 million km, composed mostly of hydrogen and helium.

Atmosphere & Climate:

Composed of layers like the photosphere, chromosphere, and corona; surface temperature ~5,500°C, core ~15 million°C.

Orbital Details:

The Sun orbits the center of the Milky Way at ~828,000 km/h, taking ~225-250 million years to complete one orbit.

Moons & Rings:

No moons or rings, but it has a powerful magnetic field and solar wind.

Notable Features:

Sunspots, solar flares, coronal mass ejections, and the solar cycle (~11 years).

Habitability & Exploration:

Supports life on Earth; studied by probes like Parker Solar Probe and SOHO.

Fun Facts:

The Sun makes up ~99.8% of the solar system’s mass, and its light takes ~8 minutes to reach Earth.
