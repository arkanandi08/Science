Saturn

Classification: Gas Giant

Discovery & History: Known since ancient times; named after the Roman god of agriculture.

Size & Structure: Second-largest planet, diameter of 116,460 km; composed mainly of hydrogen and helium, with no solid surface.

Atmosphere & Climate: Thick atmosphere with layers of ammonia clouds; extreme winds reaching up to 1,800 km/h.

Orbital Details: Orbits the Sun in 29.5 Earth years; rotates rapidly (one day lasts about 10.7 hours).

Moons & Rings: Over 146 known moons, including Titan, the second-largest moon in the solar system; has the most extensive and beautiful ring system.

Notable Features: Iconic rings made of ice and rock, hexagonal storm at the north pole, and an extremely low density (could float in water if a big enough ocean existed).

Habitability & Exploration: Uninhabitable, but Titan has conditions that could support life; explored by Pioneer, Voyager, and Cassini-Huygens missions.
