Mercury

Classification:

Terrestrial Planet

Discovery & History:

Known since ancient times; named after the Roman messenger god due to its fast orbit.

Size & Structure:

Smallest planet in the solar system, diameter of 4,880 km, composed mainly of iron and silicate rock.

Atmosphere & Climate:

Thin exosphere of oxygen, sodium, and hydrogen; extreme temperatures from -173°C at night to 427°C during the day.

Orbital Details:

Closest planet to the Sun; completes an orbit in 88 Earth days but rotates very slowly (one day lasts ~59 Earth days).

Moons & Rings:

No moons or rings.

Notable Features:

Heavily cratered surface, large Caloris Basin, and the presence of ice in shadowed craters at the poles.

Habitability & Exploration:

No possibility of life; explored by Mariner 10 and MESSENGER spacecraft.

Fun Facts:

Mercury has the greatest temperature variation in the solar system and experiences double sunrises in some locations due to its unique orbit.
