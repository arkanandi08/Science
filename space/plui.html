Pluto

Classification: Dwarf Planet

Discovery & History: Discovered by Clyde Tombaugh in 1930; originally considered the ninth planet, reclassified as a dwarf planet in 2006.

Size & Structure: Diameter of 2,377 km, smaller than Earth’s Moon; composed mostly of rock and ice.

Atmosphere & Climate: Thin atmosphere made of nitrogen, methane, and carbon monoxide; surface temperatures around -229°C.

Orbital Details: Orbits the Sun in 248 Earth years; has a highly elliptical and tilted orbit, sometimes closer to the Sun than Neptune.

Moons & Rings: 5 known moons, including Charon, which is nearly half Pluto’s size.

Notable Features: Heart-shaped Tombaugh Regio, icy mountains, frozen plains, and possible subsurface ocean.

Habitability & Exploration: Extremely cold and inhospitable; explored by NASA’s New Horizons spacecraft in 2015.

Fun Facts: Pluto’s orbit is so long that it hasn’t completed a full trip around the Sun since its discovery!
