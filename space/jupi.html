Jupiter

Classification:

Gas Giant

Discovery & History:

Known since ancient times; named after the Roman king of the gods.

Size & Structure:

Largest planet in the solar system, diameter of 139,820 km; composed mostly of hydrogen and helium, with no solid surface.

Atmosphere & Climate:

Thick atmosphere with swirling clouds of ammonia and hydrogen; home to the Great Red Spot, a massive storm.

Orbital Details:

Orbits the Sun in 11.86 Earth years; rotates extremely fast (one day lasts about 10 hours).

Moons & Rings:

At least 79 known moons, including Ganymede (largest in the solar system); faint ring system.

Notable Features:

The Great Red Spot, powerful storms, strong magnetic field, and intense radiation belts.

Habitability & Exploration:

Uninhabitable due to extreme pressure and temperature; explored by Pioneer, Voyager, Galileo, and Juno missions.

Fun Facts:

Jupiter is so massive that it could fit all the other planets inside it, and it protects Earth by deflecting comets and asteroids.
