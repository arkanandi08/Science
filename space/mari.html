Mars

Classification:

Terrestrial planet

Discovery & History:

Known since ancient times, Mars was named after the Roman god of war and has been observed by various civilizations.

Size & Structure:

Diameter of about 6,779 km; composed primarily of iron-rich rocks and dust, giving it a reddish appearance.

Atmosphere & Climate:

Thin atmosphere, mostly carbon dioxide (~95%), with temperatures ranging from -140°C to 30°C.

Orbital Details:

Orbits the Sun at an average distance of ~227.9 million km, taking ~687 Earth days to complete one orbit.

Moons & Rings:

Two small moons, Phobos and Deimos; no rings.

Notable Features:

Olympus Mons (largest volcano in the solar system), Valles Marineris (deep canyon system), polar ice caps.

Habitability & Exploration:

No known life, but potential for microbial life; extensively explored by rovers like Curiosity, Perseverance, and orbiters like Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter.

Fun Facts:

Mars has the largest dust storms in the solar system, and a day on Mars (a sol) is about 24 hours and 37 minutes.
