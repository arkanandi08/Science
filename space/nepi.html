Neptune

Classification:

Ice Giant

Discovery & History:

Discovered in 1846 by Johann Galle based on mathematical predictions; named after the Roman god of the sea.

Size & Structure:

Eighth-largest planet, diameter of 49,244 km; composed mainly of hydrogen, helium, and icy elements like water, ammonia, and methane.

Atmosphere & Climate:

Thick atmosphere with hydrogen, helium, and methane, giving it a deep blue color; has the strongest winds in the solar system, reaching up to 2,100 km/h.

Orbital Details:

Orbits the Sun in 164.8 Earth years; rotates once every 16 hours.

Moons & Rings:

14 known moons, including Triton, which orbits in the opposite direction of Neptune’s rotation; has faint, dusty rings.

Notable Features:

The Great Dark Spot, a massive storm similar to Jupiter’s Great Red Spot; extreme weather patterns and high-speed jet streams.

Habitability & Exploration:

Uninhabitable due to its icy, high-pressure environment; visited only once by Voyager 2 in 1989.

Fun Facts:

Neptune is the windiest planet, and it takes sunlight about 4 hours to reach it from the Sun!
