Ruthenium

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Ru
  • Atomic Number: 44
  • Atomic Mass: 101.07 amu
  • Group: 8 (Transition Metals)
  • Period: 5
  • Block: d-block
  • Electron Configuration: (1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2 3p6 3d10 4s2 4p6 4d7 5s1)
  • Valence Electrons: 8
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Silvery-white, hard metal with a metallic luster.
  • Density: 12.37 g/cm³
  • Melting Point: 2334°C (4233°F)
  • Boiling Point: 4150°C (7502°F)
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Conductivity: Excellent conductor of heat and electricity.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Highly resistant to corrosion and oxidation, even at high temperatures.
    • Forms compounds with oxygen, halogens, and sulfur.
  2. Oxidation States: Commonly +2, +3, +4, +6, and +8.
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~0.001 ppm (extremely rare).
  2. Commonly found in ores with other platinum group metals, such as:
    • Pyroxenite deposits
    • Pentlandite (nickel sulfide ores)
Uses of Ruthenium:-
  1. Electronics: Used in electrical contacts, resistors, and as a coating for electrical components due to its conductivity and durability.
  2. Catalysts: Acts as a catalyst in various chemical reactions, including ammonia production and hydrogenation processes.
  3. Alloys: Added to platinum and palladium alloys to increase hardness and wear resistance.
  4. Data Storage: Used in magnetic hard drives to improve storage density.
  5. Medicine: Ruthenium-based compounds are being explored as anti-cancer drugs.
Biological Role:-
  1. No Known Biological Role: Ruthenium is not essential for life.
  2. Potential Medical Applications: Ruthenium complexes are studied for their potential in cancer treatment.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Corrosion Resistance: Highly resistant to acids and oxidation, making it valuable in harsh environments.
  2. Rare Metal: One of the rarest elements in the Earth's crust.
  3. Catalytic Properties: Effective in promoting chemical reactions, especially in hydrogenation and oxidation.
History of Ruthenium:-
  1. Discovery:
    • Discovered in 1844 by Karl Ernst Claus, a Russian chemist.
    • Named after "Ruthenia," the Latin name for Russia, where it was discovered.
  2. Modern Applications:
    • Became important in electronics and catalysis in the 20th century.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Durability: Ruthenium's resistance to wear makes it ideal for long-lasting electrical contacts.
  2. Magnetic Properties: Ruthenium plays a role in increasing the storage density of hard drives.
  3. Anti-Cancer Potential: Ruthenium-based drugs show promise in treating cancer with fewer side effects than traditional treatments.
