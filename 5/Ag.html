Silver

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Ag
  • Atomic Number: 47
  • Atomic Mass: 107.87 amu
  • Group: 11 (Transition Metals)
  • Period: 5
  • Block: d-block
  • Electron Configuration: (1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2 3p6 3d10 4s2 4p6 4d10 5s1)
  • Valence Electrons: 1
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Lustrous, white, metallic.
  • Density: 10.49 g/cm³
  • Melting Point: 961.78°C (1763.2°F)
  • Boiling Point: 2162°C (3924°F)
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Conductivity: The best conductor of heat and electricity among all elements.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Low reactivity compared to other transition metals.
    • Resistant to corrosion and oxidation but tarnishes when exposed to sulfur compounds, forming silver sulfide (Ag2S).
  2. Oxidation States: Commonly +1, rarely +2 and +3.
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~0.075 ppm (rare).
  2. Found in nature in both native form (pure silver) and in ores such as:
    • Argentite (Ag2S)
    • Galena (PbS with silver impurities)
    • Cerargyrite (AgCl)
Uses of Silver:-
  1. Jewelry and Silverware: Widely used for ornaments, cutlery, and decorative items.
  2. Electronics: Essential for electrical contacts, conductors, and circuit boards due to its high conductivity.
  3. Photography: Previously used in photographic films (silver halides).
  4. Medicine: Used in antimicrobial applications, wound dressings, and dental alloys.
  5. Coins: Historically used in coinage and still in some bullion coins.
  6. Solar Panels: Plays a key role in photovoltaic cells for solar energy production.
Biological Role:-
  1. No Known Biological Role: Silver is not essential for life.
  2. Antimicrobial Properties: Silver ions (Ag+) are effective in killing bacteria and fungi.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Highest Conductivity: Silver has the highest electrical and thermal conductivity of all elements.
  2. Reflectivity: Has the highest reflectivity of any metal, making it ideal for mirrors and coatings.
  3. Antimicrobial: Widely used for its ability to kill bacteria without being harmful to human tissues.
History of Silver:-
  1. Discovery:
    • Known since ancient times, with evidence of use dating back to 3000 BCE in Anatolia (modern-day Turkey).
    • The name "silver" comes from the Anglo-Saxon word *seolfor*; the symbol "Ag" is derived from the Latin word *argentum*.
  2. Historical Significance:
    • Highly valued in ancient civilizations for its rarity, beauty, and utility in trade and currency.
    • Played a critical role in the economies of the Roman and Spanish empires.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Symbol Origin: "Ag" comes from *argentum*, meaning "white or shining" in Latin.
  2. Antibacterial Properties: Silver nanoparticles are used in modern medical and industrial applications for their antibacterial qualities.
  3. Use in Space: Silver is used in specialized equipment for space exploration due to its superior conductivity.
