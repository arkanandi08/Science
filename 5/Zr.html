Zirconium

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Zr
  • Atomic Number: 40
  • Atomic Mass: 91.22 amu
  • Group: 4 (Transition Metals)
  • Period: 5
  • Block: d-block
  • Electron Configuration: (1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2 3p6 3d10 4s2 4p6 4d2 5s2)
  • Valence Electrons: 4
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Silvery-white, shiny, and corrosion-resistant metal.
  • Density: 6.52 g/cm³
  • Melting Point: 1855°C (3371°F)
  • Boiling Point: 4409°C (7968°F)
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Conductivity: Moderate conductor of heat and electricity.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Resistant to corrosion, especially by acids and alkalis.
    • Reacts with oxygen to form zirconium dioxide (ZrO2).
    • Combines with halogens at high temperatures to form zirconium halides.
  2. Corrosive Behavior: Forms a stable oxide layer, making it highly resistant to further oxidation.
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~0.016% (160 ppm).
  2. Commonly found in minerals such as:
    • Zircon (ZrSiO4)
    • Baddeleyite (ZrO2)
Uses of Zirconium:-
  1. Nuclear Industry: Used as cladding for fuel rods in nuclear reactors due to its low neutron absorption cross-section.
  2. Ceramics: Zirconium dioxide (zirconia) is used in ceramics, dental crowns, and thermal barrier coatings.
  3. Alloys: Added to steel and other metals to improve corrosion resistance.
  4. Jewelry: Cubic zirconia is a popular synthetic gemstone resembling diamonds.
  5. Chemical Industry: Used in pumps, valves, and heat exchangers exposed to corrosive materials.
Biological Role:-
  1. Non-Essential Element: Zirconium has no known biological role in humans or animals.
  2. Biocompatibility: Zirconium is non-toxic and is often used in medical implants.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Corrosion Resistance: Highly resistant to chemical corrosion, especially by acids.
  2. Low Neutron Absorption: Ideal for use in nuclear reactors.
  3. Cubic Zirconia: A widely used synthetic gemstone with optical properties similar to diamonds.
History of Zirconium:-
  1. Discovery:
    • Discovered in 1789 by German chemist Martin Heinrich Klaproth in the mineral zircon.
    • Isolated as a pure metal in 1824 by Swedish chemist Jöns Jakob Berzelius.
  2. Modern Applications:
    • Widely used in nuclear technology, ceramics, and jewelry industries.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Zirconium is named after the mineral zircon, which has been known since ancient times.
  2. Pure zirconium metal is highly ductile and malleable.
  3. Cubic zirconia is a cost-effective alternative to diamonds, widely used in jewelry.
