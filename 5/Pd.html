Palladium

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Pd
  • Atomic Number: 46
  • Atomic Mass: 106.42 amu
  • Group: 10 (Transition Metals)
  • Period: 5
  • Block: d-block
  • Electron Configuration: (1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2 3p6 3d10 4s2 4p6 4d10)
  • Valence Electrons: 10
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Silvery-white, shiny, and metallic.
  • Density: 12.02 g/cm³
  • Melting Point: 1554.9°C (2830.82°F)
  • Boiling Point: 2963°C (5365°F)
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Conductivity: Excellent conductor of heat and electricity.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Highly resistant to corrosion and oxidation in air.
    • Reacts with halogens and acids like aqua regia.
  2. Oxidation States: Commonly +2 and +4.
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~0.015 ppm (very rare).
  2. Commonly found in ores with other platinum group metals, such as:
    • Sulfide deposits in Russia, South Africa, and Canada.
    • Nickel and copper mining byproducts.
Uses of Palladium:-
  1. Automotive Catalysts: Widely used in catalytic converters to reduce harmful emissions from vehicles.
  2. Electronics: Used in electrical contacts and as a component in multilayer ceramic capacitors.
  3. Jewelry: Used in white gold alloys and as a primary metal for fine jewelry.
  4. Hydrogen Storage: Absorbs hydrogen gas efficiently and is used in hydrogen purification processes.
  5. Fuel Cells: Plays a role in fuel cell technology, particularly in electrodes.
Biological Role:-
  1. No Known Biological Role: Palladium is not essential for life.
  2. Toxicity: Palladium compounds can be toxic if ingested or inhaled.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Hydrogen Absorption: Can absorb up to 900 times its own volume of hydrogen, making it critical in hydrogen storage and purification.
  2. Corrosion Resistance: Does not tarnish easily, even in harsh environments.
  3. Rarity: A highly sought-after precious metal due to its rarity and properties.
History of Palladium:-
  1. Discovery:
    • Discovered in 1803 by William Hyde Wollaston, a British chemist.
    • Named after the asteroid Pallas, which was discovered around the same time.
  2. Modern Applications:
    • Became prominent in the 20th century with the rise of catalytic converters and industrial uses.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Precious Metal: Palladium is one of the most valuable precious metals, often surpassing gold in price.
  2. Industrial Demand: A significant portion of palladium's demand comes from the automotive industry.
  3. Hydrogen Economy: Its ability to store and purify hydrogen positions it as a key material for future energy technologies.
