Indium

General Information:-
  • Symbol: In
  • Atomic Number: 49
  • Atomic Mass: 114.82 amu
  • Group: 13 (Post-transition Metals)
  • Period: 5
  • Block: p-block
  • Electron Configuration: (1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2 3p6 3d10 4s2 4p6 4d10 5s2 5p1)
  • Valence Electrons: 3
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Silvery-white and soft metallic.
  • Density: 7.31 g/cm³
  • Melting Point: 156.6°C (313.88°F)
  • Boiling Point: 2072°C (3762°F)
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Conductivity: Good conductor of electricity.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Relatively stable in air but can form an oxide layer over time.
    • Reacts with halogens to form indium halides.
    • Dissolves in acids, releasing hydrogen gas.
  2. Oxidation States: +3 (most common), +1 (less common).
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~0.25 ppm (rare).
  2. Found in nature primarily in zinc and lead ores, as a byproduct of their extraction, particularly in:
    • Sphalerite (ZnS)
    • Chalcopyrite (CuFeS2)
Uses of Indium:-
  1. Indium Tin Oxide (ITO): Widely used in touchscreens, LCDs, and solar panels.
  2. Semiconductors: Used in alloys for transistors and diodes.
  3. Solders: Low-melting-point indium alloys are used in specialty soldering applications.
  4. Coatings: Applied as a thin layer to prevent corrosion on metal surfaces.
  5. Cryogenics: Indium's ductility at low temperatures makes it useful for cryogenic applications.
Biological Role:-
  1. No Known Biological Role: Indium is not essential for life.
  2. Toxicity: Indium compounds are considered low in toxicity but can cause lung irritation if inhaled.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Soft Metal: Indium is so soft it can be cut with a knife.
  2. Ductility: Remains highly ductile even at very low temperatures.
  3. Audible Sound: When bent, indium emits a high-pitched "cry," similar to tin or gallium.
History of Indium:-
  1. Discovery:
    • Discovered in 1863 by Ferdinand Reich and Hieronymous Theodor Richter in Germany.
    • Named after the indigo-blue spectral line observed during its analysis.
  2. Industrial Use:
    • Became important in the 20th century with the development of electronic devices and displays.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Symbol Origin: "In" is derived from its name, Indium, based on the indigo-blue spectral line.
  2. Low Melting Point: Has one of the lowest melting points among metals, making it useful for special alloys.
  3. Flexible Applications: Used in advanced technology like photovoltaics and touchscreens.
