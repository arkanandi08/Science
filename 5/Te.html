Tellurium

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Te
  • Atomic Number: 52
  • Atomic Mass: 127.60 amu
  • Group: 16 (Chalcogens)
  • Period: 5
  • Block: p-block
  • Electron Configuration: (1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2 3p6 3d10 4s2 4p6 4d10 5s2 5p4)
  • Valence Electrons: 6
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Brittle, silvery-white metalloid with a metallic luster.
  • Density: 6.24 g/cm³
  • Melting Point: 449.51°C (841.12°F)
  • Boiling Point: 988°C (1810°F)
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Conductivity: Semi-conductor; conductivity increases with exposure to light.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Reacts with halogens to form tellurium halides (e.g., TeCl4).
    • Reacts with strong acids and alkalis.
    • Burns in air to form tellurium dioxide (TeO2).
  2. Oxidation States: -2, +2, +4, +6 (most common).
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~1 ppm (very rare).
  2. Often found in combination with metals in ores such as tellurides (e.g., calaverite, a gold telluride).
Uses of Tellurium:-
  1. Alloys:
    • Added to lead to improve strength, hardness, and resistance to corrosion.
  2. Semiconductors:
    • Used in thermoelectric devices and solar panels.
  3. Rubber Industry:
    • Used as a vulcanizing agent and to improve the durability of rubber.
  4. Optics:
    • Used in infrared optical materials.
Biological Role:-
  1. No Known Biological Role: Tellurium is not essential for life.
  2. Toxicity: Tellurium and its compounds are toxic in large amounts and can cause a garlic-like odor in breath and sweat when ingested or inhaled.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Rare Metalloid: Tellurium is one of the rarest elements in Earth's crust.
  2. Semi-Conductive: Its electrical conductivity increases when exposed to light, making it useful in electronic applications.
History of Tellurium:-
  1. Discovery:
    • Discovered in 1782 by Franz-Joseph Müller von Reichenstein in gold ores from Transylvania.
  2. Naming:
    • Named "Tellurium" in 1798 by Martin Heinrich Klaproth, derived from the Latin word *tellus*, meaning Earth.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Symbol Origin: "Te" comes from its name, Tellurium.
  2. Thermoelectric Properties: Tellurium is widely used in thermoelectric devices for converting heat into electricity.
  3. Distinct Smell: Tellurium exposure can cause a garlic-like odor in humans due to the formation of organotellurium compounds.
