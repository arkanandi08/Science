Tin

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Sn
  • Atomic Number: 50
  • Atomic Mass: 118.71 amu
  • Group: 14 (Post-transition Metals)
  • Period: 5
  • Block: p-block
  • Electron Configuration: (1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2 3p6 3d10 4s2 4p6 4d10 5s2 5p2)
  • Valence Electrons: 4
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Silvery-white, soft, and malleable metal.
  • Density: 7.31 g/cm³
  • Melting Point: 231.93°C (449.47°F)
  • Boiling Point: 2602°C (4716°F)
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Conductivity: Good conductor of electricity.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Resists corrosion from water but can be attacked by acids and alkalis.
    • Reacts with oxygen to form tin oxides (SnO, SnO2).
  2. Oxidation States: +2, +4 (most common).
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~2.3 ppm (relatively rare).
  2. Found in nature primarily in the form of the mineral cassiterite (SnO2).
Uses of Tin:-
  1. Alloys:
    • Used to make bronze (tin and copper) and pewter.
  2. Coating:
    • Used to coat other metals, such as steel, to prevent corrosion (tin cans).
  3. Soldering:
    • Widely used in soldering for electronic and plumbing applications.
  4. Glass Industry:
    • Used in the production of float glass, providing a smooth surface.
Biological Role:-
  1. No Known Biological Role: Tin is not essential for life.
  2. Toxicity: Generally low toxicity, but some organotin compounds can be harmful.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Phase Transformation: At low temperatures (below 13.2°C), tin can transform into a brittle gray allotrope, known as "tin pest."
  2. Non-toxic: Pure tin is non-toxic, making it suitable for food packaging.
History of Tin:-
  1. Ancient Use:
    • Used since ancient times to make bronze (copper-tin alloy), dating back to ~3000 BCE.
  2. Industrial Use:
    • Became essential during the Industrial Revolution for its anti-corrosive properties and use in alloys.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Symbol Origin: "Sn" comes from the Latin word *stannum*, meaning tin.
  2. Float Glass: The tin bath is critical in producing flat, smooth glass sheets.
  3. Soft Metal: Tin is soft enough to be bent and molded easily.
