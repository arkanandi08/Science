Technetium

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Tc
  • Atomic Number: 43
  • Atomic Mass: 98 (most stable isotope)
  • Group: 7 (Transition Metals)
  • Period: 5
  • Block: d-block
  • Electron Configuration: (1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2 3p6 3d10 4s2 4p6 4d5 5s2)
  • Valence Electrons: 7
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Silvery-gray, lustrous metal.
  • Density: 11 g/cm³
  • Melting Point: 2157°C (3915°F)
  • Boiling Point: 4265°C (7709°F)
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Conductivity: Good conductor of electricity and heat.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Reacts with oxygen to form technetium oxides (e.g., TcO2, TcO3).
    • Forms various compounds, including technetium halides and pertechnetates (TcO4-).
  2. Oxidation States: Commonly +4, +5, +6, and +7.
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Occurrence: Rarely found naturally. Small amounts are produced in uranium ores through spontaneous fission.
  2. Synthetic Production: First artificially created element, made by bombarding molybdenum with deuterons or neutrons.
Uses of Technetium:-
  1. Medical Imaging: Widely used in nuclear medicine for diagnostic imaging, especially as technetium-99m (a metastable isotope).
  2. Catalysts: Used as a catalyst in some chemical reactions.
  3. Corrosion Studies: Employed in studying corrosion resistance in steel.
Biological Role:-
  1. No Known Biological Role: Technetium has no natural biological function in living organisms.
  2. Radioactive Toxicity: Its radioactive isotopes can be harmful if ingested or inhaled.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. First Synthetic Element: Technetium was the first element to be artificially produced in a laboratory.
  2. Radioactivity: All isotopes of technetium are radioactive, with technetium-99 being the most stable.
  3. Diagnostic Imaging: Technetium-99m is one of the most commonly used radioactive tracers in medical diagnostics.
History of Technetium:-
  1. Discovery:
    • Predicted by Dmitri Mendeleev in the periodic table as "eka-manganese."
    • First synthesized in 1937 by Carlo Perrier and Emilio Segrè by bombarding molybdenum with deuterons.
  2. Origin of the Name: The name "technetium" comes from the Greek word *technetos*, meaning "artificial."
  3. Modern Applications: Used in nuclear medicine and corrosion-resistant alloys.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Artificial Element: It was the first element to be discovered in a lab rather than naturally occurring.
  2. Radioactive Half-Life: Technetium-99 has a half-life of 211,100 years, making it relatively long-lived for radioactive elements.
  3. Medical Importance: Technetium-99m is used in over 30 million medical procedures annually worldwide.
