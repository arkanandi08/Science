Iodine

General Information:-
  • Symbol: I
  • Atomic Number: 53
  • Atomic Mass: 126.90 amu
  • Group: 17 (Halogens)
  • Period: 5
  • Block: p-block
  • Electron Configuration: (1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2 3p6 3d10 4s2 4p6 4d10 5s2 5p5)
  • Valence Electrons: 7
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Lustrous, purple-black solid that sublimates into a violet gas.
  • Density: 4.933 g/cm³
  • Melting Point: 113.7°C (236.66°F)
  • Boiling Point: 184.3°C (363.7°F)
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Conductivity: Poor conductor of electricity and heat.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Less reactive than chlorine and bromine but reacts with metals to form iodides (e.g., KI).
    • Dissolves in water with limited solubility but readily dissolves in organic solvents like ethanol and chloroform.
  2. Oxidation States: -1, +1, +3, +5, +7.
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~0.46 ppm (rare).
  2. Commonly found in:
    • Seaweed and marine organisms.
    • Brine deposits and saltpeter (caliche) ores.
Uses of Iodine:-
  1. Medical Field:
    • Used as an antiseptic in tincture of iodine.
    • Component of contrast agents in medical imaging.
  2. Nutritional Supplements:
    • Added to table salt as potassium iodide to prevent iodine deficiency and goiter.
  3. Chemical Reagents:
    • Used in the synthesis of organic and inorganic compounds.
  4. Photography:
    • Historically used in silver iodide for photographic film.
Biological Role:-
  1. Essential Element: Iodine is crucial for thyroid gland function and the production of hormones like thyroxine (T4) and triiodothyronine (T3).
  2. Deficiency:
    • Iodine deficiency can lead to goiter and hypothyroidism.
    • Excess iodine can also cause thyroid dysfunction.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Halogen Behavior: Among the halogens, iodine is the least reactive and the most metallic in behavior.
  2. Sublimation: Iodine sublimates directly from solid to violet gas when heated.
History of Iodine:-
  1. Discovery:
    • Discovered in 1811 by Bernard Courtois, a French chemist, while extracting sodium carbonate from seaweed ash.
  2. Naming:
    • The name "Iodine" comes from the Greek word *iodes*, meaning violet or purple, referencing its vapor color.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Symbol Origin: "I" comes from its name, Iodine.
  2. Colorful Gas: Iodine vapors are violet, making them easily recognizable.
  3. Goiter Prevention: Iodine fortification in salt has greatly reduced goiter cases worldwide.
