Californium

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Cf
  • Atomic Number: 98
  • Atomic Mass: 251 amu
  • Group: Actinides
  • Period: 7
  • Block: f-block
  • Electron Configuration: [Rn] 5f107s2
  • Valence Electrons: 3
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Silvery-white, metallic.
  • Density: 15.1 g/cm³
  • Melting Point: 900°C (1,652°F)
  • Boiling Point: Estimated around 1,740°C (3,164°F)
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Radioactive: Emits alpha particles and some neutron radiation.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Reacts with oxygen to form californium oxide (CfO2).
    • Reacts with halogens to form californium halides.
  2. Oxidation States: +3, +4.
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Not found naturally; produced synthetically in nuclear reactors.
  2. Created by neutron bombardment of curium-242 or curium-244.
Uses of Californium:-
  1. Neutron Source: Used in neutron activation analysis and radiography.
  2. Medical Applications: Helps in cancer treatment through targeted radiation.
  3. Nuclear Reactors: Used as a neutron starter for reactors.
Biological Role:-
  1. Highly radioactive and toxic.
  2. No known biological function.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Man-Made Element: First synthesized in 1950.
  2. Named After: California, where it was discovered.
  3. High Neutron Emission: One of the most efficient neutron sources.
History of Californium:-
  1. Discovery: Discovered in 1950 by Albert Ghiorso and colleagues at UC Berkeley.
  2. Production: First created by bombarding curium-242 with alpha particles.
  3. Widespread Use: Utilized in industry, medicine, and nuclear science.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Californium-252 is a powerful neutron emitter, used in detecting hidden explosives.
  2. Only small amounts of californium have ever been produced.
  3. It has been used in space exploration for neutron moisture gauges.
