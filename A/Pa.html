Protactinium

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Pa
  • Atomic Number: 91
  • Atomic Mass: 231.04 amu
  • Group: Actinides
  • Period: 7
  • Block: f-block
  • Electron Configuration: [Rn] 5f26d17s2
  • Valence Electrons: 5
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Silvery-gray metallic element.
  • Density: 15.37 g/cm³.
  • Melting Point: 1,572°C (2,862°F).
  • Boiling Point: 4,026°C (7,279°F).
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid.
  • Radioactive: Emits alpha particles.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Slowly reacts with oxygen, forming oxides.
    • Reacts with halogens to form halides.
    • Forms different oxidation states, primarily +4 and +5.
  2. Oxidation States: +3, +4, +5 (most stable is +5).
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Extremely rare in nature.
  2. Primarily found in uranium ores as a decay product of uranium-235.
Uses of Protactinium:-
  1. Scientific Research: Used in nuclear physics research.
  2. Nuclear Studies: Studied for its role in nuclear fuel cycles.
Biological Role:-
  1. Highly toxic and radioactive.
  2. Has no known biological function.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. One of the rarest naturally occurring elements.
  2. Highly radiotoxic due to strong alpha emissions.
  3. Forms a variety of complex compounds.
History of Protactinium:-
  1. Discovery: Discovered in 1913 by Kasimir Fajans and Oswald Helmuth Göhring.
  2. Isolation: First isolated in 1934 by Aristid von Grosse.
  3. Name Origin: Derived from the Greek word "protos," meaning "first," indicating its place before actinium in the decay chain.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. One of the most expensive elements due to its rarity and difficulty in extraction.
  2. Protactinium-231 has a half-life of 32,760 years.
  3. Highly reactive and difficult to handle due to its radioactivity.
