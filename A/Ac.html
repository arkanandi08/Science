Actinium

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Ac
  • Atomic Number: 89
  • Atomic Mass: 227 amu
  • Group: Actinides
  • Period: 7
  • Block: f-block
  • Electron Configuration: [Rn] 6d17s2
  • Valence Electrons: 3
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Silvery-white, tarnishes in air.
  • Density: 10.07 g/cm³
  • Melting Point: 1,050°C (1,922°F)
  • Boiling Point: 3,200°C (5,792°F)
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Radioactive: Emits alpha particles.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Highly reactive with oxygen and moisture.
    • Forms actinium oxide (Ac2O3) when exposed to air.
  2. Oxidation State: +3 (most stable).
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Rare in Earth's crust, found in trace amounts in uranium and thorium ores.
  2. Mostly produced synthetically in nuclear reactors.
Uses of Actinium:-
  1. Medical Applications: Used in targeted alpha therapy (TAT) for cancer treatment.
  2. Neutron Source: Serves as a neutron emitter in research.
  3. Scientific Research: Studied for its radioactivity and nuclear properties.
Biological Role:-
  1. Highly radioactive and toxic.
  2. No known biological function in living organisms.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. First Actinide: Actinium is the first element in the actinide series.
  2. Highly Radioactive: Emits strong radiation, making it useful in medicine and research.
  3. Short Half-Life: Actinium-227 has a half-life of about 21.8 years.
History of Actinium:-
  1. Discovery: Discovered in 1899 by Friedrich Oskar Giesel.
  2. Named After: The Greek word "aktinos," meaning beam or ray, due to its radioactivity.
  3. Modern Applications: Used in cancer treatment and nuclear research.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. One gram of actinium produces more radiation than uranium.
  2. Actinium glows blue in the dark due to its intense radioactivity.
  3. It was initially mistaken for other radioactive elements before being identified as a new element.
