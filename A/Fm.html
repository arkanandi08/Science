Fermium

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Fm
  • Atomic Number: 100
  • Atomic Mass: 257 amu
  • Group: Actinides
  • Period: 7
  • Block: f-block
  • Electron Configuration: [Rn] 5f127s2
  • Valence Electrons: 3
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Metallic, silvery (assumed, but largely unknown).
  • Density: Unknown, but estimated to be around 9.7 g/cm³.
  • Melting Point: 1,527°C (2,781°F).
  • Boiling Point: Unknown due to limited availability.
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid.
  • Radioactive: Emits alpha radiation.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Reacts with oxygen to form fermium oxide (Fm2O3).
    • Forms compounds with halogens, such as fermium chloride (FmCl3).
  2. Oxidation States: +2, +3.
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Not found naturally; produced synthetically in nuclear reactors.
  2. Created by neutron bombardment of plutonium or curium isotopes.
Uses of Fermium:-
  1. Scientific Research: Used to study heavy element behavior.
  2. Nuclear Reactions: Helps in the synthesis of superheavy elements.
Biological Role:-
  1. Highly radioactive and toxic.
  2. No known biological function.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Man-Made Element: First synthesized in 1952.
  2. Named After: Enrico Fermi.
  3. Highly Radioactive: Decays quickly, limiting practical applications.
History of Fermium:-
  1. Discovery: First detected in 1952 from the debris of a hydrogen bomb test.
  2. Production: Created by bombarding uranium or curium with neutrons.
  3. Applications: Primarily used for scientific research due to its rarity.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Fermium is extremely difficult to produce in large amounts.
  2. Only microgram quantities have ever been synthesized.
  3. Its extreme radioactivity makes it challenging to study.
