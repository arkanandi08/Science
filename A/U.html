Uranium

General Information:-
  • Symbol: U
  • Atomic Number: 92
  • Atomic Mass: 238.03 amu
  • Group: Actinides
  • Period: 7
  • Block: f-block
  • Electron Configuration: [Rn] 5f36d17s2
  • Valence Electrons: 6
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Silvery-gray metal that tarnishes in air.
  • Density: 19.05 g/cm³.
  • Melting Point: 1,132°C (2,070°F).
  • Boiling Point: 4,131°C (7,468°F).
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid.
  • Radioactive: All isotopes are radioactive.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Slowly reacts with oxygen to form uranium oxides.
    • Dissolves in acids but is resistant to alkalis.
    • Forms various oxidation states, most common being +6, +5, and +4.
  2. Oxidation States: +6 (most common), +5, +4, +3.
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Found naturally in Earth's crust, seawater, and some minerals.
  2. Commonly found in uranium ores such as uraninite and pitchblende.
Uses of Uranium:-
  1. Nuclear Fuel: Used in nuclear power plants and reactors.
  2. Weapons: Enriched uranium is used in nuclear weapons.
  3. Radiation Shielding: Depleted uranium is used in shielding for radiation protection.
  4. Ammunition: Depleted uranium is used in armor-piercing rounds.
Biological Role:-
  1. No biological function.
  2. Exposure can cause kidney damage and is hazardous due to radioactivity.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Uranium-235 is fissile and can sustain a nuclear chain reaction.
  2. Uranium-238 can be converted into plutonium-239, which is also fissile.
  3. One of the heaviest naturally occurring elements.
History of Uranium:-
  1. Discovery: Discovered in 1789 by Martin Heinrich Klaproth.
  2. Name Origin: Named after the planet Uranus.
  3. Early Uses: Used to color glass and ceramics before its radioactive properties were known.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Uranium was first used for nuclear power in the 20th century.
  2. Natural uranium is 99.3% uranium-238 and only 0.7% uranium-235.
  3. Depleted uranium is used in military applications and radiation shielding.
