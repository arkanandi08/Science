Americium

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Am
  • Atomic Number: 95
  • Atomic Mass: 243 amu
  • Group: Actinides
  • Period: 7
  • Block: f-block
  • Electron Configuration: [Rn] 5f77s2
  • Valence Electrons: 3
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Silvery-white, tarnishes in air.
  • Density: 12 g/cm³
  • Melting Point: 1,176°C (2,149°F)
  • Boiling Point: 2,600°C (4,712°F)
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Radioactive: Emits alpha and gamma radiation.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Reacts with oxygen to form americium oxide (AmO2).
    • Reacts with halogens to form americium halides.
  2. Oxidation States: +3, +4, +5, +6.
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Not naturally found on Earth; produced synthetically in nuclear reactors.
  2. Created by neutron bombardment of plutonium-239.
Uses of Americium:-
  1. Smoke Detectors: Americium-241 is widely used in ionization smoke detectors.
  2. Industrial Gauging: Used in measuring material thickness and density.
  3. Scientific Research: Used as a neutron source in research applications.
Biological Role:-
  1. Highly radioactive and toxic.
  2. Can accumulate in bones if ingested, posing health risks.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Man-Made Element: First synthesized in 1944 by Glenn T. Seaborg and his team.
  2. Named After: America, following the naming pattern of europium.
  3. Long Half-Life: Americium-241 has a half-life of 432.2 years.
History of Americium:-
  1. Discovery: Discovered in 1944 during the Manhattan Project.
  2. Named By: Glenn T. Seaborg.
  3. First Application: Smoke detectors in the 1960s.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Americium-241 is used in household smoke detectors worldwide.
  2. It was the fourth synthetic transuranium element discovered.
  3. Americium emits both alpha and gamma radiation, making it useful for industrial applications.
