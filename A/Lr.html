Lawrencium

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Lr
  • Atomic Number: 103
  • Atomic Mass: 266 amu (estimated)
  • Group: Actinides
  • Period: 7
  • Block: d-block (formerly considered f-block)
  • Electron Configuration: [Rn] 5f147s27p1
  • Valence Electrons: 3
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Presumed silvery or metallic.
  • Density: Unknown, estimated to be around 15.6 g/cm³.
  • Melting Point: ~1,627°C (2,961°F) (estimated).
  • Boiling Point: Unknown due to limited availability.
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid.
  • Radioactive: Emits alpha radiation.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Reacts with oxygen to form lawrencium oxides.
    • Can form halide compounds such as lawrencium chloride (LrCl3).
  2. Oxidation States: +3 (most stable), possibly +2.
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Not found naturally; produced synthetically in particle accelerators.
  2. Created by bombarding californium-249 with boron-10 or boron-11 nuclei.
Uses of Lawrencium:-
  1. Scientific Research: Used in studying actinide and transactinide chemistry.
  2. Nuclear Science: Helps in understanding element stability and electron behavior.
Biological Role:-
  1. Highly radioactive and toxic.
  2. No known biological function.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Last Actinide: The final element of the actinide series.
  2. Named After: Ernest O. Lawrence, inventor of the cyclotron.
  3. Short-Lived: Most isotopes decay rapidly, limiting applications.
History of Lawrencium:-
  1. Discovery: First synthesized in 1961 by a team at the Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory.
  2. Production: Created using high-energy ion bombardment techniques.
  3. Applications: Primarily used for fundamental research due to its scarcity.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Lawrencium is one of the least studied elements due to its rarity.
  2. It was initially misidentified as an f-block element but later reassigned to the d-block.
  3. Its most stable isotope, Lr-262, has a half-life of about 3.6 hours.
