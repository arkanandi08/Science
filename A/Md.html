Mendelevium

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Md
  • Atomic Number: 101
  • Atomic Mass: 258 amu (estimated)
  • Group: Actinides
  • Period: 7
  • Block: f-block
  • Electron Configuration: [Rn] 5f137s2
  • Valence Electrons: 3
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Presumed silvery or metallic.
  • Density: Unknown, estimated around 10.3 g/cm³.
  • Melting Point: ~827°C (1,521°F) (estimated).
  • Boiling Point: Unknown due to limited availability.
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid.
  • Radioactive: Emits alpha radiation.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Reacts with oxygen and halogens to form compounds like mendelevium chloride (MdCl3).
    • Can exist in multiple oxidation states, with +3 being the most stable.
  2. Oxidation States: +3 (most stable), occasionally +2.
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Not found naturally; produced synthetically in particle accelerators.
  2. Created by bombarding einsteinium-253 with alpha particles (helium nuclei).
Uses of Mendelevium:-
  1. Scientific Research: Used in studying actinide chemistry and nuclear reactions.
  2. Nuclear Science: Helps in understanding element stability and transuranium behavior.
Biological Role:-
  1. Highly radioactive and toxic.
  2. No known biological function.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Named After: Dmitri Mendeleev, creator of the periodic table.
  2. First Heavily Studied Transuranium Element: One of the first actinides extensively researched after its discovery.
  3. Short-Lived: Most isotopes decay rapidly, limiting practical applications.
History of Mendelevium:-
  1. Discovery: First synthesized in 1955 by a team led by Albert Ghiorso at the University of California, Berkeley.
  2. Production: Created by bombarding einsteinium-253 with alpha particles using a cyclotron.
  3. Applications: Primarily used for scientific research due to its rarity.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Mendelevium was the first element to be synthesized one atom at a time.
  2. Its discovery confirmed the existence of actinides beyond einsteinium.
  3. The most stable isotope, Md-258, has a half-life of about 51.5 days.
