Berkelium

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Bk
  • Atomic Number: 97
  • Atomic Mass: 247 amu
  • Group: Actinides
  • Period: 7
  • Block: f-block
  • Electron Configuration: [Rn] 5f97s2
  • Valence Electrons: 3
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Silvery-white, metallic.
  • Density: 14.78 g/cm³
  • Melting Point: 986°C (1,807°F)
  • Boiling Point: Estimated around 2,620°C (4,748°F)
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid
  • Radioactive: Emits alpha radiation.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Reacts with oxygen to form berkelium oxide (BkO2).
    • Reacts with halogens to form berkelium halides.
  2. Oxidation States: +3, +4.
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Not found naturally; produced synthetically in nuclear reactors.
  2. Created by neutron bombardment of curium-244 or curium-245.
Uses of Berkelium:-
  1. Scientific Research: Used in the synthesis of heavier elements like californium.
  2. Nuclear Studies: Helps in studying actinide chemistry.
Biological Role:-
  1. Highly radioactive and toxic.
  2. No known biological function.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Man-Made Element: First synthesized in 1949.
  2. Named After: The city of Berkeley, California.
  3. Scarcity: Extremely rare and difficult to produce.
History of Berkelium:-
  1. Discovery: Discovered in 1949 by Glenn T. Seaborg and colleagues at UC Berkeley.
  2. Production: First created by bombarding americium-241 with alpha particles.
  3. Limited Applications: Mainly used for research purposes.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Only a few milligrams of berkelium have ever been produced.
  2. Its synthesis was crucial for the discovery of heavier elements like lawrencium.
  3. Berkelium’s chemistry is similar to terbium and curium.
