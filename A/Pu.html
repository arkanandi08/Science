Plutonium

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Pu
  • Atomic Number: 94
  • Atomic Mass: [244] amu (most stable isotope)
  • Group: Actinides
  • Period: 7
  • Block: f-block
  • Electron Configuration: [Rn] 5f67s2
  • Valence Electrons: 6
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Silvery-gray metal, tarnishes when exposed to air.
  • Density: 19.86 g/cm³ (higher than lead).
  • Melting Point: 639.4°C (1,183°F).
  • Boiling Point: 3,228°C (5,842°F).
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid.
  • Radioactive: Highly radioactive and emits alpha particles.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Reacts with oxygen, forming plutonium oxide (PuO2).
    • Can form various oxidation states (+3 to +7), most stable is +4.
    • Reacts with hydrogen, nitrogen, and halogens.
  2. Oxidation States: +3, +4, +5, +6, +7 (most stable is +4).
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Rare in nature, primarily found in uranium ores in trace amounts.
  2. Mainly produced synthetically in nuclear reactors by neutron bombardment of uranium-238.
Uses of Plutonium:-
  1. Nuclear Fuel: Used in nuclear reactors and nuclear weapons.
  2. Space Exploration: Plutonium-238 is used in radioisotope thermoelectric generators (RTGs) for space probes.
  3. Research: Used in scientific studies and advanced nuclear physics research.
Biological Role:-
  1. Highly toxic and radioactive.
  2. Can accumulate in bones and cause radiation damage.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Undergoes self-heating due to radioactive decay.
  2. Can exist in multiple allotropes with different properties.
  3. One of the most dangerous radioactive materials due to its long half-life and high toxicity.
History of Plutonium:-
  1. Discovery: Discovered in 1940 by Glenn T. Seaborg, Joseph W. Kennedy, and colleagues at the University of California, Berkeley.
  2. Production: First produced by bombarding uranium-238 with deuterons.
  3. Role in History: Played a key role in the development of nuclear weapons during World War II (Manhattan Project).
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Named after the dwarf planet Pluto, following the naming trend of uranium (U) and neptunium (Np).
  2. Plutonium-239 has a half-life of 24,100 years and is used in nuclear weapons.
  3. Emits faint blue glow due to radiation interaction with surrounding air.
