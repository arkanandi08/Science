Scandium

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Sc
  • Atomic Number: 21
  • Atomic Mass: 44.96 amu
  • Group: 3 (Transition Metals)
  • Period: 4
  • Block: d-block
  • Electron Configuration: (1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2 3p6 3d1 4s2)
  • Valence Electrons: 3
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Silvery-white metal with a slight yellowish tint.
  • Density: 2.985 g/cm³.
  • Melting Point: 1,541°C (2,806°F).
  • Boiling Point: 2,836°C (5,137°F).
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid.
  • Conductivity: Good conductor of heat and electricity.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Slowly reacts with air to form a yellow or pink oxide layer (Sc2O3).
    • Reacts with acids to produce hydrogen gas.
  2. Oxidation States:
    • Exhibits a +3 oxidation state in most compounds.
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~0.0024% (31st most abundant element).
  2. Common Sources:
    • Found in minerals like thortveitite (Sc2Si2O7), euxenite, and gadolinite.
    • Present in trace amounts in rare-earth ores.
Uses of Scandium:-
  1. Aerospace Industry:
    • Used in lightweight, high-strength aluminum-scandium alloys for aircraft and spacecraft.
  2. Sports Equipment:
    • Used in high-performance sports gear like baseball bats, bicycle frames, and golf clubs.
  3. Lighting:
    • Used in high-intensity discharge (HID) lamps for stadiums and film production.
  4. Nuclear Applications:
    • Scandium is used in nuclear reactors due to its low neutron absorption cross-section.
Biological Role:-
  1. No Known Biological Role:
    • Scandium is not essential for life and has no known biological role in humans or animals.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Rare and Expensive: Scandium is one of the rarest transition metals and is difficult to extract from its ores.
  2. Alloying Agent: Small amounts of scandium can significantly improve the strength and corrosion resistance of aluminum alloys.
  3. Unique Lighting: Scandium iodide is used to enhance the color quality of light in lamps.
History of Scandium:-
  1. Discovery:
    • Discovered in 1879 by Lars Fredrik Nilson, a Swedish chemist, while analyzing the mineral euxenite.
  2. Naming:
    • Named "Scandium" after Scandinavia, where it was first identified.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Predicted by Dmitri Mendeleev: Mendeleev predicted the existence of scandium in 1869, calling it "ekaboron" based on its position in the periodic table.
  2. High Cost: Scandium is very expensive due to its scarcity and the difficulty of extraction, with prices often exceeding $1,500 per kilogram.
  3. High-Strength Alloys: Even a small addition of scandium to aluminum can greatly enhance its properties, making it valuable in advanced industries.
