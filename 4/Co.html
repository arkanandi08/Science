Cobalt

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Co
  • Atomic Number: 27
  • Atomic Mass: 58.933 amu
  • Group: 9 (Transition Metals)
  • Period: 4
  • Block: d-block
  • Electron Configuration: (1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2 3p6 3d7 4s2)
  • Valence Electrons: 2
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Lustrous, silver-gray metal with a bluish tint.
  • Density: 8.86 g/cm³.
  • Melting Point: 1,495°C (2,723°F).
  • Boiling Point: 2,927°C (5,301°F).
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid.
  • Magnetic Properties: Ferromagnetic (retains magnetic properties).
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Moderately reactive, resists oxidation in air but reacts with strong acids.
    • Forms compounds like cobalt(II) chloride (CoCl2) and cobalt(II,III) oxide (Co3O4).
  2. Oxidation States:
    • Common oxidation states are +2 and +3.
  3. Compound Formation:
    • Used in the production of cobalt-based pigments, such as cobalt blue.
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~0.0029% (relatively rare).
  2. Common Sources:
    • Found in ores like cobaltite (CoAsS) and erythrite (Co3(AsO4)2).
    • Often obtained as a byproduct of copper and nickel mining.
Uses of Cobalt:-
  1. Batteries:
    • Used in lithium-ion and rechargeable batteries.
  2. Alloys:
    • Forms superalloys for jet engines and turbines.
  3. Pigments:
    • Used in ceramics and glass for its brilliant blue color (cobalt blue).
  4. Catalysts:
    • Acts as a catalyst in industrial chemical reactions.
  5. Medical Applications:
    • Used in radiotherapy and as part of vitamin B12.
Biological Role:-
  1. Essential Element:
    • A component of vitamin B12, necessary for human health.
  2. Enzyme Function:
    • Involved in various enzymatic reactions in living organisms.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Magnetic Properties: Cobalt is ferromagnetic, making it useful in magnets.
  2. Blue Pigments: Known for producing vibrant blue colors in art and ceramics.
  3. Radioactive Isotope: Cobalt-60 is used in radiotherapy and industrial radiography.
History of Cobalt:-
  1. Discovery:
    • Known since ancient times, formally identified by Georg Brandt in 1735.
  2. Ancient Uses:
    • Used to create blue glass and ceramics as early as 2,000 BCE.
  3. Modern Applications:
    • Widely used in high-tech industries and renewable energy applications.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Symbol Origin: The symbol "Co" comes from the German word *kobalt*, meaning "goblin," as early miners associated the ore with harmful spirits.
  2. Isotope Applications: Cobalt-60 is a key isotope used in cancer treatment and sterilization.
  3. Magnetism: Cobalt is one of the three naturally magnetic elements, alongside iron and nickel.
