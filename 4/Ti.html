Titanium

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Ti
  • Atomic Number: 22
  • Atomic Mass: 47.87 amu
  • Group: 4 (Transition Metals)
  • Period: 4
  • Block: d-block
  • Electron Configuration: (1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2 3p6 3d2 4s2)
  • Valence Electrons: 4
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Silvery-gray metal, lustrous and strong.
  • Density: 4.506 g/cm³.
  • Melting Point: 1,668°C (3,034°F).
  • Boiling Point: 3,287°C (5,949°F).
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid.
  • Conductivity: Poor conductor of electricity but resistant to corrosion.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Resistant to corrosion, including by seawater, aqua regia, and chlorine.
    • Reacts with oxygen at high temperatures to form titanium dioxide (TiO2).
  2. Oxidation States:
    • Exhibits oxidation states of +2, +3, and +4, with +4 being the most common.
  3. Compound Formation:
    • Forms stable compounds like titanium dioxide (TiO2) and titanium tetrachloride (TiCl4).
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~0.6% (9th most abundant element).
  2. Common Sources:
    • Found in minerals such as ilmenite (FeTiO3) and rutile (TiO2).
Uses of Titanium:-
  1. Aerospace Industry:
    • Used in aircraft, spacecraft, and missiles due to its high strength-to-weight ratio and corrosion resistance.
  2. Medical Applications:
    • Used in prosthetics, implants, and surgical instruments due to its biocompatibility.
  3. Construction:
    • Used in buildings and bridges for its durability and aesthetic appeal.
  4. Pigments:
    • Titanium dioxide (TiO2) is widely used as a white pigment in paints, plastics, and cosmetics.
  5. Energy:
    • Used in the manufacture of components for nuclear reactors.
Biological Role:-
  1. No Known Biological Role:
    • Titanium is not essential for life and has no known biological role in humans or animals.
  2. Biocompatibility:
    • Non-toxic and does not react with human tissues, making it ideal for implants.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Strength-to-Weight Ratio: Titanium is as strong as steel but 45% lighter.
  2. Corrosion Resistance: Highly resistant to corrosion, even in harsh environments like seawater.
  3. Biocompatibility: Does not react with human tissue or bodily fluids, making it suitable for medical applications.
History of Titanium:-
  1. Discovery:
    • Discovered in 1791 by Reverend William Gregor, an English clergyman, in the mineral ilmenite.
  2. Naming:
    • Named "Titanium" by Martin Heinrich Klaproth after the Titans of Greek mythology.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. High Melting Point: Titanium has one of the highest melting points among transition metals.
  2. Space Applications: Used extensively in spacecraft due to its lightweight and durability in extreme conditions.
  3. Widely Used Pigment: Titanium dioxide is the most widely used white pigment in the world.
