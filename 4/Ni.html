Nickel

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Ni
  • Atomic Number: 28
  • Atomic Mass: 58.693 amu
  • Group: 10 (Transition Metals)
  • Period: 4
  • Block: d-block
  • Electron Configuration: (1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2 3p6 3d8 4s2)
  • Valence Electrons: 2
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Lustrous, silvery-white metal.
  • Density: 8.908 g/cm³.
  • Melting Point: 1,455°C (2,651°F).
  • Boiling Point: 2,913°C (5,275°F).
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid.
  • Magnetic Properties: Weakly magnetic at room temperature.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Resists oxidation and corrosion due to a protective oxide layer.
    • Reacts slowly with acids to produce hydrogen gas.
  2. Oxidation States:
    • Common oxidation states are +2 and +3.
  3. Compound Formation:
    • Forms compounds like nickel(II) chloride (NiCl2) and nickel(II) sulfate (NiSO4).
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~0.008%.
  2. Common Sources:
    • Found in ores like pentlandite [(Ni,Fe)9S8] and garnierite [(Ni,Mg)3Si2O5(OH)4].
    • Nickel is also found in meteorites, often combined with iron.
Uses of Nickel:-
  1. Alloys:
    • Used in stainless steel and other corrosion-resistant alloys.
  2. Batteries:
    • A key component in rechargeable nickel-cadmium (NiCd) and nickel-metal hydride (NiMH) batteries.
  3. Coins:
    • Used in coinage for its durability and corrosion resistance.
  4. Catalysts:
    • Acts as a catalyst in chemical reactions like hydrogenation.
  5. Plating:
    • Nickel plating provides a protective and decorative finish.
Biological Role:-
  1. Essential Element:
    • Plays a role in the enzymes of some microorganisms.
  2. Toxicity:
    • Excessive exposure to nickel can cause health issues like allergic reactions and respiratory problems.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Corrosion Resistance: Forms a stable oxide layer, making it highly resistant to corrosion.
  2. Alloy Formation: Enhances strength, toughness, and corrosion resistance in alloys.
  3. Meteorite Presence: Often found in high concentrations in iron meteorites.
History of Nickel:-
  1. Discovery:
    • Identified by Axel Fredrik Cronstedt in 1751 while examining the mineral niccolite (NiAs).
  2. Ancient Uses:
    • Nickel-containing alloys were used in coins and tools as early as 3,500 BCE.
  3. Modern Applications:
    • Widely used in industrial, technological, and consumer applications.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Symbol Origin: The symbol "Ni" is derived from the German word *kupfernickel*, meaning "devil's copper."
  2. Magnetic Properties: Nickel is one of the three naturally magnetic elements, alongside iron and cobalt.
  3. Corrosion Resistance: Used in marine applications for its resistance to seawater corrosion.
