Bromine

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Br
  • Atomic Number: 35
  • Atomic Mass: 79.90 amu
  • Group: 17 (Halogens)
  • Period: 4
  • Block: p-block
  • Electron Configuration: (1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2 3p6 3d10 4s2 4p5)
  • Valence Electrons: 7
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Reddish-brown liquid with a strong odor.
  • Density: 3.12 g/cm³ (liquid).
  • Melting Point: -7.2°C (19°F).
  • Boiling Point: 58.8°C (137.8°F).
  • State at Room Temperature: Liquid (the only non-metallic element that is liquid at room temperature).
  • Conductivity: Poor electrical conductor.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Oxidation States:
    • Common oxidation states are -1, +1, +3, +5, and +7.
  2. Reactivity:
    • Highly reactive, especially with metals to form bromides.
    • Reacts with hydrogen to form hydrogen bromide (HBr).
  3. Compound Formation:
    • Forms compounds like sodium bromide (NaBr) and bromine trifluoride (BrF3).
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~2.5 ppm (parts per million).
  2. Sources:
    • Found in seawater as bromide ions (Br-).
    • Extracted from brine pools and salt deposits.
Uses of Bromine:-
  1. Flame Retardants:
    • Used in the production of brominated flame retardants.
  2. Photography:
    • Silver bromide (AgBr) is used in photographic films.
  3. Water Treatment:
    • Used as a disinfectant in water treatment and swimming pools.
  4. Medicinal Applications:
    • Used in some medications and sedatives.
Biological Role:-
  1. Trace Element:
    • Required in trace amounts for certain biological processes in some organisms.
  2. Toxicity:
    • Highly toxic to humans in large quantities and can cause irritation to skin and mucous membranes.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Only Liquid Non-Metal:
    • Unique among non-metals as a liquid at room temperature.
  2. Strong Odor:
    • Has a sharp and unpleasant smell.
History of Bromine:-
  1. Discovery:
    • Discovered in 1826 by Antoine Jérôme Balard and Carl Löwig.
  2. Name Origin:
    • Derived from the Greek word "bromos," meaning stench, due to its strong odor.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Bromine compounds were historically used in dyes and as an anti-knock agent in leaded gasoline.
  2. It is less reactive than chlorine but more reactive than iodine.
  3. Bromine's red vapor is highly corrosive and dangerous to inhale.
