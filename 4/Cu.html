Copper

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Cu
  • Atomic Number: 29
  • Atomic Mass: 63.546 amu
  • Group: 11 (Transition Metals)
  • Period: 4
  • Block: d-block
  • Electron Configuration: (1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2 3p6 3d10 4s1)
  • Valence Electrons: 1
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Reddish-orange, shiny metal.
  • Density: 8.96 g/cm³.
  • Melting Point: 1,085°C (1,984°F).
  • Boiling Point: 2,562°C (4,644°F).
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid.
  • Conductivity: Excellent conductor of heat and electricity.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Resists corrosion due to the formation of a protective oxide layer.
    • Reacts with oxygen to form copper oxide (CuO) and copper(I) oxide (Cu2O).
    • Reacts with acids like nitric acid and sulfuric acid.
  2. Oxidation States:
    • Common oxidation states are +1 and +2.
  3. Compound Formation:
    • Forms compounds like copper sulfate (CuSO4) and copper chloride (CuCl2).
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~0.0068%.
  2. Common Sources:
    • Found in ores like chalcopyrite (CuFeS2), malachite (Cu2(CO3)(OH)2), and cuprite (Cu2O).
    • Also found in native form as metallic copper in some locations.
Uses of Copper:-
  1. Electrical Applications:
    • Widely used in electrical wiring and components due to its excellent conductivity.
  2. Plumbing:
    • Used in pipes and fittings for its corrosion resistance.
  3. Alloys:
    • Used in alloys like bronze (copper and tin) and brass (copper and zinc).
  4. Coins:
    • Used in coinage for its durability and corrosion resistance.
  5. Chemical Applications:
    • Used as a catalyst in chemical reactions and in the production of fungicides.
Biological Role:-
  1. Essential Element:
    • Plays a key role in enzymes and biological processes, including iron metabolism and antioxidant defense.
  2. Toxicity:
    • Excessive exposure to copper can cause health issues, such as gastrointestinal distress.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. High Conductivity: Second only to silver in electrical and thermal conductivity.
  2. Corrosion Resistance: Forms a green patina (copper carbonate) over time, protecting it from further corrosion.
  3. Recyclability: Highly recyclable without losing its properties.
History of Copper:-
  1. Discovery:
    • Known since ancient times; evidence of use dates back over 10,000 years.
  2. Ancient Uses:
    • Used to make tools, weapons, and jewelry in the Bronze Age.
  3. Modern Applications:
    • Extensively used in electrical, industrial, and construction applications.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Symbol Origin: The symbol "Cu" comes from the Latin word *cuprum*, meaning "metal of Cyprus."
  2. Oldest Metal: Copper is one of the first metals to be used by humans, dating back to prehistoric times.
  3. Antimicrobial Properties: Copper surfaces have natural antimicrobial properties, killing bacteria and viruses upon contact.
