Gallium

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Ga
  • Atomic Number: 31
  • Atomic Mass: 69.723 amu
  • Group: 13 (Post-transition Metals)
  • Period: 4
  • Block: p-block
  • Electron Configuration: (1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2 3p6 3d10 4s2 4p1)
  • Valence Electrons: 3
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Soft, silvery-blue metal.
  • Density: 5.91 g/cm³.
  • Melting Point: 29.76°C (85.57°F).
  • Boiling Point: 2,204°C (3,999°F).
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid (but melts just above room temperature).
  • Conductivity: Good conductor of heat and electricity.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Reacts with acids and alkalis to form gallium salts.
    • Forms an oxide layer when exposed to air.
  2. Oxidation States:
    • The most common oxidation state is +3.
  3. Compound Formation:
    • Forms compounds like gallium arsenide (GaAs) and gallium chloride (GaCl3).
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~0.0018%.
  2. Common Sources:
    • Found in trace amounts in ores like bauxite and sphalerite.
Uses of Gallium:-
  1. Semiconductors:
    • Used in gallium arsenide (GaAs) for LEDs, solar panels, and high-speed electronics.
  2. Thermometers:
    • Used in high-temperature thermometers as a non-toxic mercury substitute.
  3. Medical Applications:
    • Gallium compounds are used in pharmaceuticals and imaging agents.
  4. Alloys:
    • Used to form low-melting-point alloys.
Biological Role:-
  1. No known biological role for gallium in humans.
  2. However, gallium salts have shown promise in medical treatments for cancer and infections.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Low Melting Point: Gallium melts at just above room temperature, allowing it to melt in your hand.
  2. Expands Upon Solidification: Gallium expands when it freezes, unlike most metals.
  3. Non-toxic: Gallium is relatively non-toxic and safe to handle.
History of Gallium:-
  1. Discovery:
    • Discovered in 1875 by French chemist Paul-Émile Lecoq de Boisbaudran using spectroscopy.
  2. Name Origin:
    • Named after "Gallia," the Latin name for France.
  3. Predicted by Mendeleev:
    • Dmitri Mendeleev predicted the existence and properties of gallium before its discovery, calling it "eka-aluminum."
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Gallium can be supercooled below its melting point and remain a liquid.
  2. It is one of the few metals that can alloy with most other metals.
  3. Gallium is used in mirrors as a reflective coating.
