Manganese

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Mn
  • Atomic Number: 25
  • Atomic Mass: 54.938 amu
  • Group: 7 (Transition Metals)
  • Period: 4
  • Block: d-block
  • Electron Configuration: (1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2 3p6 3d5 4s2)
  • Valence Electrons: 7
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Silvery-gray, brittle metal.
  • Density: 7.21 g/cm³.
  • Melting Point: 1,246°C (2,275°F).
  • Boiling Point: 2,061°C (3,742°F).
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid.
  • Conductivity: Moderate conductor of heat and electricity.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Combines with oxygen, sulfur, and halogens.
    • Forms a wide range of oxidation states (+2 to +7).
  2. Oxidation States:
    • Common oxidation states are +2, +4, and +7.
    • Potassium permanganate (KMnO4) contains Mn in the +7 oxidation state.
  3. Compound Formation:
    • Forms manganese dioxide (MnO2) and manganese sulfate (MnSO4).
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~0.1% (12th most abundant element).
  2. Common Sources:
    • Found in minerals such as pyrolusite (MnO2) and rhodochrosite (MnCO3).
    • Major producers include South Africa, Australia, and China.
Uses of Manganese:-
  1. Steel Production:
    • Used as an alloying agent to improve strength and corrosion resistance.
  2. Batteries:
    • Manganese dioxide is used in alkaline batteries.
  3. Glass and Ceramics:
    • Used to remove greenish tints from glass.
  4. Fertilizers:
    • Manganese compounds are used in agricultural fertilizers to enhance plant growth.
  5. Oxidizing Agent:
    • Potassium permanganate is used as a disinfectant and water purifier.
Biological Role:-
  1. Essential Trace Element:
    • Manganese is crucial for enzymes involved in metabolism, bone formation, and antioxidant activity.
  2. Toxicity:
    • Excessive manganese exposure can cause neurological disorders.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Oxidation Versatility: Manganese exhibits a wide range of oxidation states.
  2. Hardening Agent: Essential in steelmaking to improve strength and toughness.
  3. Coloring Agent: Manganese compounds are responsible for purple and pink hues in glass and ceramics.
History of Manganese:-
  1. Discovery:
    • Recognized as an element in 1774 by Swedish chemist Johan Gottlieb Gahn, who isolated it from pyrolusite.
  2. Naming:
    • Derived from the Latin word *magnesia* (from the mineral magnesite).
  3. Industrial Use:
    • Widely used since the 19th century in steel production and as a coloring agent.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Oxidizing Power: Potassium permanganate is a powerful oxidizing agent used in chemical reactions and water purification.
  2. Abundant in Minerals: Manganese is never found as a free element in nature but is abundant in mineral form.
  3. Essential for Life: Trace amounts of manganese are critical for human health and enzyme function.
