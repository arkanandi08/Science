Arsenic

General Information:-
  • Symbol: As
  • Atomic Number: 33
  • Atomic Mass: 74.92 amu
  • Group: 15 (Metalloids)
  • Period: 4
  • Block: p-block
  • Electron Configuration: (1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2 3p6 3d10 4s2 4p3)
  • Valence Electrons: 5
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Gray, metallic-looking solid.
  • Density: 5.73 g/cm³.
  • Melting Point: Sublimes at 613°C (1,135°F) without melting at atmospheric pressure.
  • Boiling Point: 817°C (1,503°F) (at higher pressures).
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid.
  • Conductivity: Poor electrical conductor in elemental form but conducts when alloyed.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Oxidation States:
    • Common oxidation states are -3, +3, and +5.
  2. Compound Formation:
    • Forms compounds like arsenic trioxide (As2O3) and arsenic pentoxide (As2O5).
  3. Reactivity:
    • Stable in dry air but tarnishes in moist air.
    • Reacts with halogens and concentrated acids.
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~1.8 ppm (parts per million).
  2. Sources:
    • Found in minerals like arsenopyrite, realgar, and orpiment.
    • Often associated with sulfide ores of other metals.
Uses of Arsenic:-
  1. Pesticides and Herbicides:
    • Arsenic compounds are used in agricultural chemicals (though restricted in many countries).
  2. Alloys:
    • Used in alloys to improve hardness and corrosion resistance.
  3. Electronics:
    • Gallium arsenide (GaAs) is used in semiconductors and LED production.
  4. Medical Applications:
    • Historically used in medicine, such as Fowler's solution, though now rare due to toxicity.
Biological Role:-
  1. Toxic Element:
    • Highly toxic to humans and other living organisms.
    • Small quantities of arsenic are required by some species for growth.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Sublimation:
    • Arsenic transitions directly from solid to gas when heated under standard conditions.
  2. Poisonous Nature:
    • Known for its toxicity and historically used as a poison.
  3. Allotropic Forms:
    • Exists in several forms, including gray, yellow, and black arsenic.
History of Arsenic:-
  1. Discovery:
    • Known since ancient times and identified as a distinct element in 1250 by Albertus Magnus.
  2. Uses in History:
    • Used as a poison and in medicinal applications throughout history.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Arsenic compounds like arsenic trioxide were historically used in rat poisons and insecticides.
  2. Napoleon Bonaparte's death has been speculated to involve arsenic poisoning.
  3. Gallium arsenide is a key material in modern electronic devices.
