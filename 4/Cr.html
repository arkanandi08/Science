Chromium

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Cr
  • Atomic Number: 24
  • Atomic Mass: 51.996 amu
  • Group: 6 (Transition Metals)
  • Period: 4
  • Block: d-block
  • Electron Configuration: (1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2 3p6 3d5 4s1)
  • Valence Electrons: 6
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Lustrous, hard, and silvery-gray metal.
  • Density: 7.19 g/cm³.
  • Melting Point: 1,907°C (3,465°F).
  • Boiling Point: 2,671°C (4,840°F).
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid.
  • Conductivity: Good conductor of heat and electricity.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Forms a protective oxide layer, making it corrosion-resistant.
    • Reacts with oxygen, halogens, and acids under specific conditions.
  2. Oxidation States:
    • Exhibits oxidation states ranging from -2 to +6, with +3 and +6 being the most common.
  3. Compound Formation:
    • Forms compounds such as chromium(III) oxide (Cr2O3) and potassium dichromate (K2Cr2O7).
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~0.014% (21st most abundant element).
  2. Common Sources:
    • Found in minerals such as chromite (FeCr2O4).
    • Major deposits are located in South Africa, Kazakhstan, and India.
Uses of Chromium:-
  1. Stainless Steel:
    • Used to produce stainless steel due to its corrosion-resistant properties.
  2. Plating:
    • Used for chrome plating to enhance durability and appearance.
  3. Pigments:
    • Compounds like chromium oxide are used as pigments in paints and ceramics.
  4. Refractories:
    • Used in making refractory bricks for high-temperature applications.
  5. Catalysis:
    • Chromium compounds are used as catalysts in various chemical reactions.
Biological Role:-
  1. Essential Element:
    • Chromium(III) is essential in small amounts for glucose metabolism in humans.
  2. Toxicity:
    • Hexavalent chromium (Cr6+) is toxic and a known carcinogen.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Corrosion Resistance: Chromium's protective oxide layer makes it highly resistant to corrosion.
  2. Colorful Compounds: Chromium compounds display vivid colors, such as the green of chromium oxide and the orange of potassium dichromate.
  3. High Strength: Chromium enhances the hardness and strength of alloys.
History of Chromium:-
  1. Discovery:
    • Discovered in 1797 by Louis-Nicolas Vauquelin, a French chemist, who isolated it from the mineral crocoite (PbCrO4).
  2. Naming:
    • Named "Chromium" from the Greek word *chroma*, meaning color, due to its colorful compounds.
  3. Industrial Significance:
    • Widely used since the 20th century for producing stainless steel and chrome plating.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Vivid Colors: Chromium compounds are used to produce green and red pigments for glass and ceramics.
  2. Hardness: Chromium is one of the hardest metals, used to strengthen alloys.
  3. Protective Layer: Chromium is used for chrome plating to enhance resistance to corrosion and wear.
