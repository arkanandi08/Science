Krypton

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Kr
  • Atomic Number: 36
  • Atomic Mass: 83.798 amu
  • Group: 18 (Noble Gases)
  • Period: 4
  • Block: p-block
  • Electron Configuration: (1s22s22p63s23p63d104s24p6)
  • Valence Electrons: 8
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Colorless gas, glows with a whitish-green or pale blue light when excited.
  • Density: 3.749 g/L (at 0°C and 1 atm).
  • Melting Point: -157.37°C (-251.27°F).
  • Boiling Point: -153.415°C (-244.147°F).
  • State at Room Temperature: Gas.
  • Conductivity: Non-conductor of electricity in normal conditions but becomes conductive in a plasma state.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Extremely low reactivity due to a full valence electron shell.
    • Forms compounds only under extreme conditions (e.g., krypton difluoride, KrF2).
  2. Inert Behavior: Resists chemical reactions under standard conditions.
  3. Flame Color: Emits a whitish-green light when electrified.
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Atmosphere: ~1 ppm (0.0001% by volume).
  2. Sources:
    • Obtained as a byproduct during the fractional distillation of liquid air.
Uses of Krypton:-
  1. Lighting: Used in fluorescent lamps, neon lights, and high-intensity discharge lamps.
  2. Lasers: Krypton-ion lasers are used in scientific research and medical applications.
  3. Photography: Krypton-filled flash tubes are used for high-speed photography.
  4. Windows: Mixed with argon in insulated glazing to improve thermal efficiency.
  5. Standards: Previously used to define the meter's length based on its spectral lines (now replaced by more precise methods).
Biological Role:-
  1. No Known Biological Role: Krypton is biologically inert and non-toxic in low concentrations.
  2. Safety: High concentrations can displace oxygen in confined spaces, posing an asphyxiation risk.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Inert Nature: One of the noble gases, krypton rarely forms compounds under normal conditions.
  2. Emission Spectrum: Emits characteristic spectral lines, making it useful in scientific applications.
  3. Density: Heavier than air, which contributes to its limited use in specialized environments.
History of Krypton:-
  1. Discovery: Discovered in 1898 by Sir William Ramsay and Morris Travers while analyzing the residue left after evaporating liquid air.
  2. Name Origin: Derived from the Greek word "kryptos," meaning "hidden."
  3. Applications Over Time: Initially a scientific curiosity, krypton gained importance in lighting and other technological applications.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Symbol Origin: "Kr" is derived from its Greek name.
  2. Rare Nature: Despite its rarity, krypton is commercially extracted from the atmosphere in small quantities.
  3. Glow: When electrified, krypton emits a distinctive whitish-green light, making it ideal for certain lighting applications.
