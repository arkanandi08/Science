Iron

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Fe
  • Atomic Number: 26
  • Atomic Mass: 55.845 amu
  • Group: 8 (Transition Metals)
  • Period: 4
  • Block: d-block
  • Electron Configuration: (1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2 3p6 3d6 4s2)
  • Valence Electrons: 2 (in 4s orbital)
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Lustrous, silvery-gray metal.
  • Density: 7.87 g/cm³.
  • Melting Point: 1,538°C (2,800°F).
  • Boiling Point: 2,862°C (5,184°F).
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid.
  • Conductivity: Good conductor of heat and electricity.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Combines with oxygen and water to form rust (hydrated iron oxide).
    • Reacts with acids to produce hydrogen gas.
  2. Oxidation States:
    • Common oxidation states are +2 (ferrous) and +3 (ferric).
    • Forms compounds like iron(II) sulfate (FeSO4) and iron(III) chloride (FeCl3).
  3. Compound Formation:
    • Forms oxides like hematite (Fe2O3) and magnetite (Fe3O4).
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~5% (4th most abundant element).
  2. Common Sources:
    • Found in ores such as hematite, magnetite, and siderite.
    • Major producers include Australia, Brazil, and China.
Uses of Iron:-
  1. Construction:
    • Used in steel production for buildings, bridges, and infrastructure.
  2. Transportation:
    • Used in automobiles, ships, and railway tracks.
  3. Machinery:
    • Essential in manufacturing tools and industrial equipment.
  4. Biological Applications:
    • Iron is crucial in hemoglobin for oxygen transport in the body.
  5. Magnets:
    • Iron and its alloys are used to make magnets.
Biological Role:-
  1. Essential Element:
    • Iron is vital for life, playing a key role in oxygen transport and enzyme activity.
  2. Hemoglobin:
    • Iron is a central component of hemoglobin in red blood cells.
  3. Deficiency:
    • Iron deficiency can lead to anemia, characterized by fatigue and weakness.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Magnetic Properties: Iron is one of the three naturally magnetic elements.
  2. Abundant in Earth's Core: Iron is the main component of Earth's core, contributing to the planet's magnetic field.
  3. Alloy Formation: Iron forms numerous alloys, including steel and cast iron.
History of Iron:-
  1. Discovery:
    • Known since ancient times and widely used during the Iron Age (~1200 BCE).
  2. Industrial Revolution:
    • The development of iron smelting and steelmaking fueled the Industrial Revolution.
  3. Modern Applications:
    • Iron and steel are indispensable materials in modern industries.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Earth's Core: Earth's core is primarily composed of iron and nickel.
  2. Symbol Origin: The symbol "Fe" comes from the Latin word *ferrum* (iron).
  3. Rusting: Iron readily reacts with oxygen and moisture, forming rust.
