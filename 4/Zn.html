Zinc

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Zn
  • Atomic Number: 30
  • Atomic Mass: 65.38 amu
  • Group: 12 (Transition Metals)
  • Period: 4
  • Block: d-block
  • Electron Configuration: (1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2 3p6 3d10 4s2)
  • Valence Electrons: 2
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Bluish-white, lustrous metal.
  • Density: 7.14 g/cm³.
  • Melting Point: 419.53°C (787.15°F).
  • Boiling Point: 907°C (1,665°F).
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid.
  • Conductivity: Good conductor of electricity and heat.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Reacts with acids to release hydrogen gas.
    • Reacts with oxygen to form zinc oxide (ZnO).
  2. Oxidation States:
    • The most common oxidation state is +2.
  3. Compound Formation:
    • Forms compounds like zinc chloride (ZnCl2) and zinc sulfate (ZnSO4).
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~0.0078%.
  2. Common Sources:
    • Found in ores like sphalerite (ZnS) and calamine (ZnCO3).
Uses of Zinc:-
  1. Galvanization:
    • Used to coat iron and steel to prevent corrosion.
  2. Alloys:
    • Used to make brass (zinc and copper) and other alloys.
  3. Construction:
    • Used in roofing materials and die-casting.
  4. Batteries:
    • Used in zinc-carbon and zinc-air batteries.
  5. Medicine:
    • Used in supplements and ointments for skin health.
Biological Role:-
  1. Essential Element:
    • Vital for enzyme function, immune system support, and cell growth.
  2. Deficiency:
    • Zinc deficiency can cause growth retardation, impaired immune function, and hair loss.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Corrosion Resistance: Zinc forms a protective layer of zinc carbonate when exposed to air.
  2. Low Melting Point: Has a relatively low melting point compared to other metals.
  3. Antibacterial Properties: Zinc oxide is widely used in antimicrobial creams and products.
History of Zinc:-
  1. Discovery:
    • Zinc has been used since ancient times, but it was first isolated as a metal in India around the 13th century.
  2. Ancient Uses:
    • Zinc compounds were used for medicinal and cosmetic purposes in ancient civilizations.
  3. Modern Applications:
    • Zinc became widely used in industry during the 19th century for galvanization and alloy production.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Symbol Origin: The symbol "Zn" comes from the German word *Zink*, meaning "pointed," due to its appearance in certain crystal forms.
  2. Galvanization: Zinc's use in galvanization accounts for nearly half of its worldwide consumption.
  3. Recyclability: Zinc is highly recyclable and retains its properties even after being reused.
