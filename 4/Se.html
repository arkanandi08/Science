Selenium

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Se
  • Atomic Number: 34
  • Atomic Mass: 78.96 amu
  • Group: 16 (Chalcogens)
  • Period: 4
  • Block: p-block
  • Electron Configuration: (1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2 3p6 3d10 4s2 4p4)
  • Valence Electrons: 6
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Lustrous gray solid, or red in its amorphous form.
  • Density: 4.81 g/cm³ (gray form).
  • Melting Point: 221°C (430°F).
  • Boiling Point: 685°C (1,265°F).
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid.
  • Conductivity: Poor electrical conductor, but conductivity increases when exposed to light.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Oxidation States:
    • Common oxidation states are -2, +4, and +6.
  2. Reactivity:
    • Reacts with halogens and forms selenides with metals.
    • Combines with oxygen to form selenium dioxide (SeO2).
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~0.05 ppm (parts per million).
  2. Sources:
    • Found in minerals such as pyrite and nickel-copper sulfide ores.
    • Often recovered as a by-product of refining copper.
Uses of Selenium:-
  1. Glass Manufacturing:
    • Used to decolorize glass and make red-colored glass.
  2. Electronics:
    • Used in photocells, light meters, and solar cells due to its photoconductive properties.
  3. Alloys:
    • Improves machinability of steel and copper alloys.
  4. Medical and Nutritional Uses:
    • Trace amounts of selenium are essential for human health, used in dietary supplements.
Biological Role:-
  1. Essential Nutrient:
    • Selenium is a crucial component of certain enzymes, such as glutathione peroxidase.
  2. Health Benefits:
    • Supports immune system and thyroid function.
    • Deficiency can lead to health issues, including Keshan disease.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Photoconductivity:
    • Conducts electricity better in the presence of light, making it useful in photocells.
  2. Allotropic Forms:
    • Exists in several forms, including amorphous red selenium and metallic gray selenium.
History of Selenium:-
  1. Discovery:
    • Discovered in 1817 by Jöns Jakob Berzelius and Johann Gottlieb Gahn while studying sulfuric acid residues.
  2. Name Origin:
    • Derived from the Greek word "selene," meaning moon.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Selenium's photoconductive properties were used in early photocopying processes.
  2. It is toxic in large amounts but essential in trace amounts for humans and animals.
  3. Named after the moon because it was discovered shortly after tellurium, which is named after the Earth.
