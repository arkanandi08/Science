Germanium

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Ge
  • Atomic Number: 32
  • Atomic Mass: 72.63 amu
  • Group: 14 (Metalloids)
  • Period: 4
  • Block: p-block
  • Electron Configuration: (1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2 3p6 3d10 4s2 4p2)
  • Valence Electrons: 4
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Lustrous, grayish-white metalloid.
  • Density: 5.323 g/cm³.
  • Melting Point: 938.25°C (1,720.85°F).
  • Boiling Point: 2,833°C (5,131°F).
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid.
  • Conductivity: Semiconductor with moderate electrical conductivity.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Oxidation States:
    • Common oxidation states are +2 and +4.
  2. Compound Formation:
    • Forms compounds like germanium dioxide (GeO2) and germanium tetrachloride (GeCl4).
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~1.5 ppm (parts per million).
  2. Sources:
    • Found in minerals like argyrodite, germanite, and sphalerite.
Uses of Germanium:-
  1. Electronics:
    • Widely used in transistors and semiconductors.
  2. Fiber Optics:
    • Used in fiber optic systems for its optical properties.
  3. Infrared Optics:
    • Used in infrared lenses and detectors.
  4. Alloys:
    • Improves strength and corrosion resistance in alloys.
Biological Role:-
  1. Non-essential Element:
    • Germanium has no known biological role in humans.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Semiconductor Properties:
    • Germanium is a key element in the development of semiconductors.
  2. Transparency to Infrared Light:
    • Germanium is transparent to infrared radiation.
History of Germanium:-
  1. Discovery:
    • Discovered in 1886 by Clemens Winkler, a German chemist.
  2. Name Origin:
    • Named after Germany, the discoverer's homeland.
  3. Predicted by Mendeleev:
    • Dmitri Mendeleev predicted its existence and properties as "eka-silicon" before its discovery.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Germanium was one of the first elements used in the development of transistors.
  2. It is often used as a component in solar cells.
