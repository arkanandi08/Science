Potassium

General Information:-
  • Symbol: K
  • Atomic Number: 19
  • Atomic Mass: 39.10 amu
  • Group: 1 (Alkali Metals)
  • Period: 4
  • Block: s-block
  • Electron Configuration: (1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2 3p6 4s1)
  • Valence Electrons: 1
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Soft, silvery-white metal.
  • Density: 0.89 g/cm³ (less dense than water).
  • Melting Point: 63.38°C (146.08°F).
  • Boiling Point: 759°C (1,398°F).
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid.
  • Conductivity: Excellent conductor of heat and electricity.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Highly reactive, especially with water and air.
    • Reacts violently with water to produce potassium hydroxide (KOH) and hydrogen gas:
    • Reacts with oxygen to form potassium oxide (K2O).
  2. Flame Color:
    • Burns with a lilac-colored flame.
  3. Corrosive Behavior:
    • Oxidizes rapidly in air, forming a white coating of potassium oxide and potassium carbonate.
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~2.4% (7th most abundant element).
  2. Common Sources:
    • Found in minerals like sylvite (KCl) and carnallite (KMgCl3·6H2O).
    • Abundant in fertilizers as potassium compounds.
Uses of Potassium:-
  1. Fertilizers:
    • Potassium compounds (e.g., potassium chloride, potassium sulfate) are essential for plant growth.
  2. Industrial Chemicals:
    • Used in the production of glass, soaps, and detergents.
  3. Medicine:
    • Potassium salts are used to treat potassium deficiencies and maintain electrolyte balance.
  4. Heat Transfer:
    • Liquid potassium is used as a coolant in specialized applications like nuclear reactors.
  5. Explosives:
    • Potassium nitrate (saltpeter) is a key component in gunpowder and fireworks.
Biological Role:-
  1. Essential Element:
    • Vital for life, as it helps regulate fluid balance, nerve signals, and muscle contractions.
  2. Cell Function:
    • Maintains cellular function and is a key electrolyte in the body.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Highly Reactive: Potassium is so reactive it must be stored in oil to prevent reactions with air or water.
  2. Low Density: It is one of the few metals lighter than water and floats when placed in it.
  3. Flame Test: Emits a distinct lilac-colored flame during a flame test.
History of Potassium:-
  1. Discovery:
    • Discovered in 1807 by Sir Humphry Davy through the electrolysis of molten potash (KOH).
  2. Naming:
    • Named "potassium" from potash, the substance from which it was first isolated.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Symbol Origin: "K" comes from the Latin word "kalium," derived from the Arabic word "alqali," meaning plant ashes.
  2. Vital in Agriculture: Potassium is one of the three primary nutrients in fertilizers, alongside nitrogen and phosphorus.
  3. Reactivity: When dropped in water, potassium reacts explosively, producing a lilac flame and hydrogen gas.
