Calcium

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Ca
  • Atomic Number: 20
  • Atomic Mass: 40.08 amu
  • Group: 2 (Alkaline Earth Metals)
  • Period: 4
  • Block: s-block
  • Electron Configuration: (1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2 3p6 4s2)
  • Valence Electrons: 2
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Silvery-white, soft metal.
  • Density: 1.55 g/cm³.
  • Melting Point: 842°C (1,548°F).
  • Boiling Point: 1,484°C (2,703°F).
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid.
  • Conductivity: Good conductor of heat and electricity.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Moderately reactive, reacts with water to form calcium hydroxide (Ca(OH)2) and hydrogen gas.
    • Reacts with oxygen to form calcium oxide (CaO).
  2. Flame Color:
    • Burns with a brick-red flame.
  3. Compound Formation:
    • Forms ionic compounds like calcium carbonate (CaCO3) and calcium chloride (CaCl2).
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~4.2% (5th most abundant element).
  2. Common Sources:
    • Found in minerals such as limestone (CaCO3), gypsum (CaSO4·2H2O), and fluorite (CaF2).
Uses of Calcium:-
  1. Construction Materials:
    • Used in cement and concrete as calcium carbonate and calcium oxide.
  2. Steel Production:
    • Used as a reducing agent to remove impurities during steel production.
  3. Food and Nutrition:
    • Calcium compounds are used as dietary supplements and fortifiers in food products.
  4. Medicine:
    • Calcium carbonate is used as an antacid and in bone-strengthening treatments.
  5. Chemical Industry:
    • Used to produce calcium carbide, which is essential for acetylene production.
Biological Role:-
  1. Essential for Life:
    • Calcium is crucial for strong bones and teeth, muscle contractions, and nerve function.
  2. Cellular Function:
    • Plays a key role in cell signaling and blood clotting.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Abundance: One of the most abundant elements in both Earth's crust and the human body.
  2. Reactivity: Reacts with water more vigorously than magnesium but less than potassium.
  3. Flame Test: Emits a brick-red flame, useful in chemical identification.
History of Calcium:-
  1. Discovery:
    • Discovered in 1808 by Sir Humphry Davy through the electrolysis of calcium oxide (lime).
  2. Naming:
    • Derived from the Latin word "calx," meaning lime.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Abundance in the Body: Calcium is the most abundant metal in the human body, primarily found in bones and teeth.
  2. Role in Construction: Calcium-based compounds like cement and plaster are the foundation of modern construction.
  3. Natural Cycle: Calcium plays a vital role in the geological carbon cycle through its presence in limestone and marine shells.
