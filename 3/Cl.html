Chlorine

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Cl
  • Atomic Number: 17
  • Atomic Mass: 35.45 amu
  • Group: 17 (Halogens)
  • Period: 3
  • Block: p-block
  • Electron Configuration: (1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2 3p5)
  • Valence Electrons: 7
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Pale greenish-yellow gas.
  • Density: 3.2 g/L (gas) at standard conditions.
  • Melting Point: -101.5°C (-150.7°F).
  • Boiling Point: -34.04°C (-29.27°F).
  • State at Room Temperature: Gas.
  • Odor: Pungent, irritating smell.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Highly reactive nonmetal and strong oxidizing agent.
    • Reacts with metals to form chlorides (e.g., NaCl).
    • Combines with hydrogen to form hydrogen chloride (HCl).
  2. Oxidation States:
    • Common oxidation states are -1, +1, +3, +5, and +7.
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~0.045%, primarily found in salt compounds.
  2. Common Sources:
    • Occurs as sodium chloride (NaCl) in seawater and rock salt.
    • Found in minerals like sylvite (KCl).
Uses of Chlorine:-
  1. Water Treatment:
    • Used to disinfect drinking water and swimming pools.
  2. Bleaching Agent:
    • Used in the production of paper and textiles.
  3. Plastics:
    • A key component in the production of polyvinyl chloride (PVC).
  4. Medicines:
    • Used in the synthesis of many pharmaceuticals.
  5. Household Cleaning:
    • Present in products like bleach and disinfectants.
Biological Role:-
  1. Essential Ion:
    • Chloride ions (Cl⁻) are vital for maintaining fluid balance and acid-base regulation in the body.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Strong Disinfectant: Kills bacteria and viruses effectively.
  2. Greenish Gas: Chlorine's distinctive pale green color makes it easily recognizable.
  3. Toxicity: Chlorine gas is highly toxic and was used as a chemical weapon in World War I.
History of Chlorine:-
  1. Discovery:
    • Discovered in 1774 by Carl Wilhelm Scheele, who identified it as a gas.
  2. Naming:
    • Named "chlorine" by Humphry Davy in 1810, derived from the Greek word "chloros," meaning greenish-yellow.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Bleach Ingredient: Chlorine is the active ingredient in most household bleach products.
  2. Swimming Pools: Responsible for the characteristic "pool smell," which is actually caused by its reaction with organic matter.
  3. Highly Reactive: Rarely found in its pure form in nature due to its high reactivity.
