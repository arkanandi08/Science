Magnesium

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Mg
  • Atomic Number: 12
  • Atomic Mass: 24.305 amu
  • Group: 2 (Alkaline Earth Metals)
  • Period: 3
  • Block: s-block
  • Electron Configuration: (1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2)
  • Valence Electrons: 2
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Shiny, silvery-white metal.
  • Density: 1.738 g/cm³.
  • Melting Point: 650°C (1,202°F).
  • Boiling Point: 1,090°C (1,994°F).
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid.
  • Conductivity: Excellent conductor of heat and electricity.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Reacts with water to form magnesium hydroxide and hydrogen gas, though reaction is slow at room temperature.
    • Burns in air with a bright white flame to form magnesium oxide (MgO).
  2. Oxidation State:
    • Exhibits a +2 oxidation state in most of its compounds.
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~2.1% by mass, the eighth most abundant element.
  2. Common Sources:
    • Found in minerals like magnesite (MgCO3) and dolomite (CaMg(CO3)2).
    • Occurs in seawater as dissolved magnesium ions (Mg2+).
Uses of Magnesium:-
  1. Alloys:
    • Used in lightweight alloys for aerospace, automotive, and electronics industries.
  2. Construction:
    • Magnesium compounds, such as magnesia (MgO), are used in refractory materials.
  3. Health and Medicine:
    • Magnesium supplements are vital for bone health, muscle function, and enzyme activity.
    • Used as an antacid and laxative (e.g., milk of magnesia).
  4. Fireworks and Flares:
    • Produces a bright white light, making it ideal for fireworks and emergency flares.
Biological Role:-
  1. Essential Element:
    • Magnesium is a critical component of chlorophyll, enabling photosynthesis in plants.
    • Vital for human health, playing a key role in muscle contraction, nerve function, and maintaining strong bones.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Lightweight Metal: Magnesium is one of the lightest structural metals, with excellent strength-to-weight ratio.
  2. Bright Combustion: Burns with an intense white flame, often used in visual displays and signaling.
  3. Abundance in Seawater: Found abundantly in seawater, making extraction economical.
History of Magnesium:-
  1. Discovery:
    • First isolated in 1808 by Sir Humphry Davy through the electrolysis of magnesium oxide and mercury oxide.
  2. Naming:
    • Named after Magnesia, a region in Greece where magnesium compounds were first discovered.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Flame Test: Produces an intense white flame during combustion.
  2. Structural Use: Magnesium alloys are used in car frames, laptops, and cameras due to their lightweight nature.
  3. Biological Importance: Magnesium ions are crucial for over 300 enzymatic reactions in the human body.
