Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Extremely inert and does not form stable compounds under normal conditions.
  2. Oxidation States:
    • Exhibits an oxidation state of 0 due to its full outer electron shell.
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Atmosphere: ~0.93% (third most abundant gas).
  2. Sources:
    • Obtained through the fractional distillation of liquid air.
Uses of Argon:-
  1. Lighting:
    • Used in incandescent and fluorescent light bulbs to prevent filament oxidation.
  2. Welding:
    • Acts as an inert shielding gas in arc welding and cutting.
  3. Electronics:
    • Used in the manufacturing of semiconductors and as a gas in plasma displays.
  4. Preservation:
    • Used to protect historical documents and materials from degradation.
  5. Scientific Research:
    • Used in laboratories and cryogenics due to its inertness.
Biological Role:-
  1. No Known Biological Role:
    • Argon is non-toxic and biologically inert.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Noble Gas: Completely inert under normal conditions.
  2. Third Most Abundant Gas: Makes up nearly 1% of Earth's atmosphere.
  3. Color in Plasma: Emits a violet or lavender glow when electrically excited.
History of Argon:-
  1. Discovery:
    • Discovered in 1894 by Lord Rayleigh and Sir William Ramsay while studying the composition of air.
  2. Naming:
    • Named "Argon" from the Greek word "argos," meaning inactive or lazy, due to its inert nature.
