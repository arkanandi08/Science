Phosphorus

General Information:-
  • Symbol: P
  • Atomic Number: 15
  • Atomic Mass: 30.974 amu
  • Group: 15 (Nitrogen Group)
  • Period: 3
  • Block: p-block
  • Electron Configuration: (1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2 3p3)
  • Valence Electrons: 5
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Exists in several forms, including white (waxy), red, and black phosphorus.
  • Density:
    • White Phosphorus: 1.82 g/cm³
    • Red Phosphorus: ~2.2–2.34 g/cm³
  • Melting Point:
    • White Phosphorus: 44.15°C (111.47°F)
    • Red Phosphorus: Sublimes at ~416°C (780°F).
  • Boiling Point: 280°C (536°F) for white phosphorus (decomposes for other forms).
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid.
  • Conductivity: Non-conductive in most forms.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • White phosphorus is highly reactive and ignites spontaneously in air.
    • Red phosphorus is less reactive but still flammable at high temperatures.
  2. Oxidation States:
    • Common oxidation states are -3, +3, and +5.
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~0.1%, primarily found as phosphate minerals.
  2. Common Sources:
    • Found in apatite minerals (e.g., fluorapatite and hydroxyapatite).
    • Occurs in guano deposits and sedimentary rocks.
Uses of Phosphorus:-
  1. Fertilizers:
    • Phosphates are essential components of agricultural fertilizers.
  2. Matches:
    • Red phosphorus is used in safety matches.
  3. Steel Production:
    • Phosphorus is used in the manufacture of certain steels and phosphor bronze alloys.
  4. Detergents:
    • Phosphates are used in detergents and cleaning agents.
  5. Military Applications:
    • White phosphorus is used in incendiary devices and smoke bombs.
Biological Role:-
  1. Essential for Life:
    • Phosphorus is a key component of DNA, RNA, and ATP (energy transfer molecule).
    • It is also a major component of bones and teeth as hydroxyapatite.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Allotropes: Exists in several forms, with vastly different properties (white, red, and black phosphorus).
  2. Highly Reactive: White phosphorus is extremely reactive and must be stored underwater or in inert gases.
  3. Luminescence: White phosphorus glows in the dark due to chemiluminescence.
History of Phosphorus:-
  1. Discovery:
    • Discovered in 1669 by Hennig Brand while distilling urine in search of gold.
  2. Naming:
    • Named after the Greek word "phosphoros," meaning "light-bearer," due to its glow.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Essential Element: No life on Earth would be possible without phosphorus.
  2. Toxicity: White phosphorus is highly toxic and can cause severe burns upon contact.
  3. Global Demand: Phosphorus is critical in agriculture, leading to concerns over phosphate rock depletion.
