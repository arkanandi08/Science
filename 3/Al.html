Aluminium

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Al
  • Atomic Number: 13
  • Atomic Mass: 26.98 amu
  • Group: 13 (Boron Group)
  • Period: 3
  • Block: p-block
  • Electron Configuration: (1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2 3p1)
  • Valence Electrons: 3
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Silvery-white, lightweight metal.
  • Density: 2.70 g/cm³.
  • Melting Point: 660.32°C (1,220.58°F).
  • Boiling Point: 2,470°C (4,478°F).
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid.
  • Conductivity: Excellent conductor of heat and electricity.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Forms a protective oxide layer, making it resistant to corrosion.
    • Reacts with acids and bases, showing amphoteric behavior.
  2. Oxidation State:
    • Typically exhibits a +3 oxidation state in compounds.
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~8.23%, the most abundant metal in the crust.
  2. Common Sources:
    • Found in bauxite ore, which contains aluminium hydroxides and oxides.
    • Also found in feldspar and other silicate minerals.
Uses of Aluminium:-
  1. Construction:
    • Used in windows, doors, roofing, and structural components.
  2. Transportation:
    • Lightweight material for aircraft, automobiles, and trains.
  3. Packaging:
    • Used in cans, foils, and food packaging due to its non-toxic nature.
  4. Electrical Applications:
    • Used in power lines and electrical cables due to its high conductivity and lightweight nature.
  5. Household Items:
    • Used in utensils, appliances, and furniture.
Biological Role:-
  1. No Known Biological Role:
    • Non-essential for life but is non-toxic in small amounts.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Lightweight and Strong: Ideal for applications where weight is a critical factor.
  2. Corrosion Resistance: Forms a thin oxide layer that protects it from rusting.
  3. Highly Recyclable: Aluminium can be recycled without losing its properties.
History of Aluminium:-
  1. Discovery:
    • First isolated in 1825 by Hans Christian Ørsted using electrolysis.
  2. Industrial Use:
    • Widespread production began in the late 19th century with the Hall-Héroult process.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Third Most Abundant Element: After oxygen and silicon, aluminium is the third most abundant element in Earth's crust.
  2. Light but Strong: Aluminium is one-third the density of steel but still strong enough for structural applications.
  3. Space Exploration: Used in spacecraft and satellites due to its strength, lightweight, and resistance to corrosion.
