Silicon

General Information:-
  • Symbol: Si
  • Atomic Number: 14
  • Atomic Mass: 28.085 amu
  • Group: 14 (Carbon Group)
  • Period: 3
  • Block: p-block
  • Electron Configuration: (1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2 3p2)
  • Valence Electrons: 4
Physical Properties:-
  • Appearance: Hard, brittle crystalline solid with a metallic luster.
  • Density: 2.329 g/cm³.
  • Melting Point: 1,410°C (2,570°F).
  • Boiling Point: 3,265°C (5,909°F).
  • State at Room Temperature: Solid.
  • Conductivity: A semiconductor; conductivity increases with temperature.
Chemical Properties:-
  1. Reactivity:
    • Forms silicon dioxide (SiO2) when exposed to oxygen at high temperatures.
    • Reacts with halogens to form silicon halides.
    • Resistant to most acids but dissolves in hydrofluoric acid.
  2. Oxidation States:
    • Exhibits +4 and, less commonly, +2 oxidation states in its compounds.
Occurrence and Abundance:-
  1. Abundance in Earth's Crust: ~27.7%, the second most abundant element after oxygen.
  2. Common Sources:
    • Occurs in minerals like quartz (SiO2), feldspar, and mica.
    • Found in silicate rocks and sands.
Uses of Silicon:-
  1. Semiconductors:
    • Used in computer chips, transistors, and solar cells.
  2. Construction:
    • Silicon dioxide (sand) is a primary component of glass and concrete.
  3. Silicones:
    • Used in lubricants, sealants, adhesives, and medical implants.
  4. Alloys:
    • Added to aluminum and steel to enhance strength and corrosion resistance.
Biological Role:-
  1. Trace Element:
    • Plays a role in bone and connective tissue health in humans.
Unique Characteristics:-
  1. Semiconductor Behavior: Essential for electronics and computing industries.
  2. Abundance: Found in nearly all rocks, sand, and soils as silicate minerals.
  3. High Thermal Stability: Makes it ideal for high-temperature applications.
History of Silicon:-
  1. Discovery:
    • Discovered by Jöns Jacob Berzelius in 1824 by heating potassium with silicon tetrafluoride (SiF4).
  2. Naming:
    • Derived from the Latin word "silex," meaning flint or hard stone.
Interesting Facts:-
  1. Silicon Valley: Named after silicon due to its critical role in the tech industry.
  2. Most Widespread Element: Silicon dioxide is a major component of sand worldwide.
  3. Solar Power: Silicon is the key material in photovoltaic cells used in solar panels.
